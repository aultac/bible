**Genesis 31:1-10 > Plans to leave and dream of spotted goats**

- PATH OF CAIN: it was because of God’s favor on Jacob that Laban became successful as well, but all his sons see is what they don’t have. They took the path of Cain to hate the success of others, even when the success of others was benefitting them!
- YOU ARE CAIN: and yet, you know the sons’ reaction is there inside you, too. Seeing Jacob get wealthier and wealthier all while he was supposed to be helping your Dad.
- SO GOD MADE THEM SPOTTED: it wasn’t sticks after all, unless God told him about the sticks as a visual sign to the people around Jacob.
- JACOB HAD HIS PART: But we learned earlier that Jacob was actively trying to get all the good sheep for himself with his sticks and stronger vs. weaker breeding. No wonder Laban doesn’t look on him with favor. Laban’s sons may have a point, even if it is ultimately God’s doing.

**Genesis 31:11-13 > Angel of the LORD**

- ANGEL OF YAHWEH SAYS HE IS GOD: and specifically the God of Bethel
- GOD IS EVERYWHERE: As in, “I am here too, Jacob, not just at the “House of God” where you said ‘Surely God is in this place.’”

**Genesis 31:14-16 > Wives agree**

- PLANNING TO TAKE THE WEALTH: they are all on board with sneaking out all their stuff
- ONLY TIME LEAH AND RACHEL AGREE: this is the only time Rachel and Leah agree on something

**Genesis 31:17-32 > Rachel’s Theft and Laban’s search**

- 300 MILES IN 10 DAYS: Jacob is especially good at running away! 30 miles/day is VERY fast for a caravan. Running from Esau was 40 miles/day!
- WHY NEITHER GOOD NOR BAD?: why couldn’t Laban say anything good? Because Laban could have enticed Jacob back to live with him via flattery so he could continue to take advantage of him. God needed Jacob in Canaan, so Laban could neither hurt him nor entice him back. **God outsmarted Laban**: he left him no conniving way around God’s intention by including “good” in the prohibition.
- SYNCRETISM: Rachel seemed to believe both in pagan gods and in God. This is called “syncretism”. Dennis Prager puts it this way: “Rachel’s behavior may have been similar to that of Niels Bohr, the Nobel prize winning physicist who was said to keep a rabbit’s foot in his laboratory. When an astonished visitor asked “But surely professor you don’t believe in a rabbit‘s foot?” Bohr responded “Of course not. But they say a rabbit’s foot brings you luck whether you believe in it or not.”
- HONOR AMONG THIEVES: Laban didn’t believe Jacob because Laban knew he himself would have lied about it. The liar complains the loudest when he is lied to. The thief thinks stealing is fine until he is stolen from.
- CAN CUT THE TENSON WITH A KNIFE: imagine Rachel’s fear. What would Laban and Jacob have done?

**Genesis 31:33-end > Laban’s search**

- GREAT IRONY: Jacob has never lost his temper with Laban before despite many legitimate grievances. But he loses his temper here in the only case where Laban was actually right
- LABAN ADMITS. he was never going to allow Jacob to leave with his family or his possessions.
- JACOB AND LABAN AGREEMENT: it is an agreement between crooks. “God watch you or you’ll steal all my stuff”.
- RIGHTEOUS PUNISHMENT OF LIARS: As Prager writes: “This is the built-in punishment of the dishonest. They go through life convinced they are constantly being deceived.” In Laban’s case, his paranoia cost him his daughters and his grandchildren, and, ultimately, a share in God’s promise.

**Genesis 32:1-12 > Jacob scared of Esau and prays**

- OUT OF THE FRYING PAN AND INTO THE FIRE: First Laban, now Esau.
- NEWS FROM HOME: somehow Jacob knew where he was living now, and then he was the head of his own nation prior to his scouts coming back and telling him of the 400 armed men
- APPARENTLY JACOB HAS NO ARMY: unlike his grandfather Abraham. Laban had the army.
- 20 YEARS OF DECEPTION: and the chickens are coming home to roost. And Jacob knows it.
- BIRTHRIGHT DOESN’T SEEM SO IMPORTANT NOW: remember graduating from high school and things that seemed SOOOO important as you sat in class and saw your friends every day suddenly weren’t important at all?
- AWAKE ALL NIGHT: worst night of Jacob’s life. There is no way out. He’s laying awake. All night, mind racing.
- JACOB’S MENTAL CHECKLIST OF GOD’S PROMISES: go through all details and see if they could all be reasonably fulfilled at this point. This is why Jacob is worried. He tells God that Esau might kill the children, thus thwarting God’s plan for his descendants. He’s not sure if God will allow Esau to kill him, so he brings in the kids.
  - The land on which you lie I will give to you and your offspring
  - Your offspring will be like the dust of the earth and shall spread abroad
  - All families of Earth blessed through you.
  - I will keep you wherever you go
  - I will bring you back to this land.
- JACOB’S PLEA ABOUT THE KIDS: God to fulfill his promised through Jacob, even if Esau killed him since Jacob has all these kids now.
- JACOB HAS DOUBTS: even if you meet God face to face, even if he directly promises you descendants and protection wherever you go, you STILL would struggle with faith when you can’t see the way though a tough time.

**Genesis 32:13-24.5 > everyone sent, Jacob alone**

- MAXIMIZE GIFT IMPACT: by spreading them out instead of all at once
- GEOGRAPHY: Esau is behind Jacob, coming up the road on the north of the Jabok because that’s the way the servants knew Jacob was traveling and Esau was chasing him on the path the servants said they were taking. Jacob was heading along the Jabok to Succoth. Jacob sends his family to the south side of the Jabok, **away from Esau to protect them, not toward Esau to shield himself.** I always used to think he used his kids and wives as a shield, but the geography (and the text) says that Jacob meets Esau first, then the wives come.

**Genesis 32:24.5-end > Jacob wrestle with God**

- WEIRD ENDING: does anybody here think that God could not overcome Jacob? Or that God could not get away from him when Jacob said he would not let go unless he blessed him?
- STORY IS CONFUSING: Is God trying to get Jacob to do something and Jacob won’t (trust in God for salvation?), or is Jacob trying to do something and God won’t let him do it (Run away)? Maybe Jacob was thinking he was going to try to fight Esau himself, so God wrestled with him directly and then maimed him to seal the deal that he could not fight Esau. What was the purpose of maiming Jacob for the rest of his life? If Jacob was trying to run away, the maiming stopped that. If Jacob needed to remember to trust in God instead of himself, the maiming would remind him every day.
- PREVAILING BY HANGING ON TO GOD: **Read Hosea 12.** Israel is proud of its heritage. God reminds them how bad Jacob was, and the only good things Jacob had came from God. The only prevailing he did was crying out to God for blessing. He had to work 14 years for a wife like a slave. Israel has nothing to be proud of in Jacob except God’s promise, which was all God’s doing. But they reject God.
- MY BEST GUESS: Remember, the story must tell us what we need to know to interpret its lesson, if there is one. The only thing it tells us is that Jacob is reduced to pleading for blessing from God. God’s blessing on Jacob is the most important thing about Jacob. So the interpretation that Jacob has learned that he must beg and plead with God for salvation from Esau and blessing, to rely solely on God and not himself, seems like it must be the right one. This is a turning point for Jacob in his relationship with God. **Victory comes through weakness, through hanging on to God and struggling with God until he is annoyed with you.** It comes through the weakness of the maimed hip.
- GOD WANTS US TO WRESTLE: bring your problems to him and wrestle. Don’t stay silent. Ask your questions. He does not chastise Jacob for the struggle.
- ISRAEL VS ISLAM: struggle vs submission.
- ADOPTED INTO ISRAEL: did you know that when you became a Christian you became adopted into the “struggle with God?”
- ISRAEL FROM HERE ON OUT: God stops promising through only one son each generation. Every single son of Jacob becomes the nation of Israel. And **they adopt the name of struggle.** “
- WHY DOES HE NOT GIVE HIS NAME? why is this Jesus‘s answer? In this story, it is an intentional mystery. The text did not provide enough information to fully understand what it meant. Why is the name of this man/god an intentionally a mystery? **Read Colossians 1:24-29, then 1:15-20.** **The mystery is Christ’s position in the trinity.**

**Genesis 33:1-11 > Esau is nice**

- WHAT WAS ESAU’S INTENTION?: kill Jacob and changed his mind, or he thought Jacob might attack him? How do you read this story? Depends on why you think Esau had 400 armed men with him.
- JACOB GIVES ESAU HIS BLESSING: he took it before, and now he is giving some of it back. This is a new side of Jacob. In the reunion of the two brothers, you might think they would be on somewhat equal footing. But Esau does not offer gifts to Jacob like Jacob offered gifts to Esau. Jacob was definitely the one that needed to make amends.
- WHAT IS THE FACE OF GOD THING ABOUT? It seems blasphemous. But if anybody knows what the face of God looks like, it is Joseph who was just at Peniel. There is a metaphorical explanation that the night before Jacob was sure he was going to die. The only thing he had they gave him any hope would be the God he wrestled with. So when he actually was delivered from certain death, it might be a little like seeing the face of God. Another explanation is **you held my life in your hands, and I deserved your wrath, and you forgave me.**
- IN THE END: brothers reconcile, Esau is nothing but kind.

**Genesis 33:12-end > Journey’s end**

- JACOB LIES TO ESAU: “if they are driven hard for one day all the flocks will die.” He’s been driving them hard, 40 miles/day, at least until Laban caught up with them.
- THERE AND BACK AGAIN: Poem from lord of the rings to help feel the magnitude of the journey from Rebekah to Laban and back?
