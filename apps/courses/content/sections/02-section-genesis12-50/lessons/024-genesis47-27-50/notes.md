They voluntarily gave up their place in the higher Egyptian aristocracy and openly identified themselves with their ‘alien’ kinsmen, the despised shepherd immigrants. Every Jewish parent may well pray that their child would show dedication to their father and their father’s God as Ephraim and Manasseh did.”

**Genesis 49:1-27 > All Son’s Blessings**

- POETRY: this is poetry, so recall that the purpose of prose is to replicate an idea or thing from brain to brain or mind to mind. The purpose of poetry is to communicate with the soul. It is the author’s attempt to recreate an experience in the soul of the hearer. It uses imagery, sounds, and an appeal to shared experiences elsewhere to attempt to get you to feel something. So when you see poetry and listen to it, ask yourself, what experience or feeling is the author going for here? This is why Biblical poetry is so hard to grasp: we do not have many shared experiences with people 4000 years ago. For example, **when they refer to a “wild donkey”, we don’t get the same feelings they did.**  They relied on donkeys for work, for travel, for carrying their burdens. They were their lifelines, a part of their everyday life from sunrise to sundown. Even worse, **some of the Hebrew words in this section only appear here and nowhere else**, so it is hard to know what they actually mean.

**Genesis 50:2-3 > Embalmed Jacob and Egyptian Weeping**

- WHY DID EGYPTIANS EMBALM? They believed after death your soul in the spiritual world would come back at night to inhabit your earthly body to refresh and recharge. If your body decayed or was destroyed, your spirit would lose its “anchor” and it would die a second time, “the second death”. Spell 175 in “the book of the dead” was titled “Spell for not dying a second time.” That terrified them more than anything.
- WHY DID JOSEPH EMBALM JACOB? He used “physicians” not “priests”: he had to travel with the body 17 days. Egypt mourned him like a Pharoah for 70 days. It’s hot in Egypt. Bodies last a few hours. Trust me the farmer, I know.

**Genesis 50:4-11 > Funeral Procession through Threshing floor of Atad**

- “SIT SHIVA”: when they close relative to someone Jewish dies, they “sit Shiva“ which means “sit seven“. They leave their regular life for seven days and sit in their house while the people they know come and sit with them. This shows that practice is an ancient: that is what Joseph and crew have done here. They sat in the morning for seven days.
- BEYOND THE JORDAN: it is on the Egyptian side of the Jordan. Scholars arguing for the Mosaic authorship of the Torah say this phrase shows Moses wrote it from the Eastern side of the Jordan, since it was “beyond the Jordan.”
