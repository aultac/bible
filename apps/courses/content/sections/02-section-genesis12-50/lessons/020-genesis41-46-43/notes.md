**Genesis 41:46-end > plenty and famine and sons and forgetting**

- GENIUS OF JOSEPH: the new “grain tax” was stored directly in the cities where it was collected. **If he had centralized it, the people would have revolted.** Instead, they all experienced the security of knowing the food they raised was still in their city.
- SONS BORN: two half-tribes of Israel mothered by the daughter of an Egyptian High Priest.
- POWER OF FORGETTING: It can’t be truly “forgetting”, because you can’t name your son about the thing you want to forget and forget it. **Don’t think about Zebras. Go**. CS Lewis’ The Great Divorce depicts a soul named “the Big Man” in hell remembering all the past wrongs done to him in life. He meets a murderer, Len, who used to work under him and is mad that Len is in heaven and not him. Len tells him he has to let go of all that from the past and ask for forgiveness for himself. That i**n heaven, all the former things are put away because everything has been made new.** The Big Man won’t do it, he doesn’t want that kind of forgetting. This is the kind of forgetting Joseph means: moving on from past wrongs done to him. It is freeing. That’s the kind of forgetting that Joseph names Manasseh.
- MEN AND WOMEN FORGETTING: I have sympathy for women in this regard. I think it is much, much harder for women to “forget” like this. Women seem to have a mental filing cabinet that logs every perceived wrong ever done to them, and when any new event comes up, the filing cabinet gets opened and the previous faults are read back into the legal record. A woman rarely argues what is happening now, they are generally arguing “now and everything that came before” from the filing cabinet. The man will often try to focus on what just happened, and the woman will resent that focus because it invalidates her feeling of the importance of the file full of past wrongs. **Most arguments between men and women have each side arguing about different things than the other side is arguing about.**  So if you find your arguments confusing, like they never go as planned, men recognize that you need to deal with the unresolved past even if you don’t want to, and women, recognize that your husband wants to focus on solving the problem at hand now and leave the unresolved problems to later. If either side can see that in the heat of the battle, the arguments start becoming more predictable. And from the outside looking in, **my plea to women is to recognize that you have a filing cabinet, and it is often making you miserable for no good reason. You need to name the filing cabinet “Manasseh”.**

**Genesis 42:1-5 > Jacob sends brothers to Egypt**

- LOVING DAD: quit standing around staring at each other and go get food in Egypt so we don’t die.
- MAP: about 340 miles from Hebron to Pharoah’s Palace. At 20 miles/day, **that’s 17 days one-way**. Walking from Fulton, Indiana to north side of Nashville, Tennessee. No books or phones: what do you talk about that whole way?
- BENJAMIN IS NOT YOUNG: we think of him as little Benjamin needing protection. **He’s around 37 years old.**

**Genesis 42:6-17 > Joseph accuses them of spying**

- DREAM FULFILLED BEFORE HIS EYES: He’s about 39 years old. He stopped thinking about his family long ago. Life turned out ok for him. Going about the daily routine selling grain at increasingly high prices. **Looks up and suddenly his first dream from 23 years ago is coming true right before his eyes,** his brothers that he barely recognizes are bowing before him. How did he keep it together?
- BROTHERS DID NOT RECOGNIZE JOSEPH: he is 39 now, Egyptian hair cut, speaking Egyptian through interpreter, most powerful man in Egypt under Pharoah. Thoroughly Egyptian. No way they could recognize.
- FLABBERGASTED BROTHERS: No way they expected any push back. They expected to buy grain and leave. They must have been flabbergasted. Speechless.
- WHY BENJAMIN? **Did Joseph think that Benjamin might be dead too?** Did he want to see his little brother from the same mother? Did he know this would be hard for them to bring Benjamin and he’s intentionally making things difficult for them? Did he want to protect Benjamin? I think he speaks to them harshly because Benjamin is not there and now he is suspicious.
- EXQUISITE IRONY: why did they say specifically “twelve brothers all sons of one man”? Ever have that happen to you where you over-share because of confusion? This is oddly specific.
- JAILED FOR 3 DAYS: imagine the nights. The days. The hours together in that cell. The conversations. The complete disbelief. **The confusion.** Joseph came up. Prayers to God. Blame. Despair. Planning. Helplessness. “When we get out we can clear this all up.”
- WHAT WOULD YOU DO? What did Joseph think those three days and nights? What plans did he scrap? What would you do? Kill them? He decided to get Benjamin and test his brothers to see if they are willing to sacrifice a brother now. **He settled on a plan to see if they’ve changed.**
- BENJAMIN?!? They came out of prison planning to clear this all up. Now Benjamin?! How did this happen? This makes no sense! Despair. Frustration.
- WHAT DOES THIS HAVE TO DO WITH BEING SPIES? How, exactly, would this prove we aren’t spies?
- I FEAR ELOHIM: through the interpreter: I fear Elohim = I fear your God. Strangely, the brothers do not mention this to Dad.
- WHY SIMEON? Joseph chose Simeon, not the brothers. The eldest, Reuben, would have been the normal choice. But Reuben tried to save him. So Simeon was second born.
- GUILT OVER JOSEPH 20 YEARS AGO: first thing that comes up. It’s been eating them all on the inside for 20 years. Admitting sin is the first step to redemption, and they finally admit it here. In front of Joseph.

**Genesis 42:18-28 > money discovered in bags**

- GOD DID IT? They blame God. They have decided this must all be punishment from God for Joseph. Ironically, it is indeed from ”God” indirectly because of what they did to Joseph.
- CONFUSION: we know the end of the story, but they didn’t. A reasonable explanation would have been some kind of palace intrigue within Egypt, warring factions using them as pawns in some game of power. There aren’t very many rational explanations that you can even come up with.

**Genesis 42:29-end > Jacob will not let them go back with Benjamin**

- IMAGINE JACOB’S CONFUSION: Every time you boys leave things go wrong. Makes no sense at all. Are the boys lying? But here’s the grain. Here’s the money. Simeon is gone. Nothing makes sense. **None of the boys would have believed it either if they hadn’t been there themselves.**
- RUEBEN’S OFFER: Dad, you’ve never believed in me, but I can do this. Rueben is the only one acting like a Dad should here. I will save Simeon and bring Benjamin back. Ironically, he does this at the pledge of his own son’s lives. Sort of like “I swear on the lives of my two children.” Jacob remembers 35:22 Rueben slept with Bilhah and refuses Rueben, probably rightly so.
- FALSE CONFIDENCE: Realistically, there’s **no way Ruben should have any confidence in his own ability**. He could not stand against the might of Egypt to guarantee Benjamin‘s return. Nothing on the first trip was in his control. He failed to save Joseph from being sold in the first place. He was in charge this time and we lost Simeon. Why would the second trip be any different?
- BENJAMIN IS THE ONLY ONE LEFT: **what a cruel way for a father to treat sons**. He is saying I never wanted your mom. I never wanted you. Benjamin is the only one left that I wanted. I’ll send all of you to Egypt because I don’t care if you come back. I don’t care if Simeon comes back. But Benjamin stays here alone.

- JOSEPH WAS FULLY EGYPTIAN: had priest of On’s daughter as wife, spoke Egyptian, dressed Egyptian, ate Egyptian, and had an Egyptian name. An Egyptian name assigned by Pharoah would be citizenship.

They refuse to even touch someone who has eaten cow. Now imagine the shock of the Hebrews 400 years later when God tells them to burn the gods of Egypt (cows) as sacrifice to him. Daily watching the smoke of the gods of their former captors ascend to heaven.

- ALL GROUPS GRAVITATE TO SELF-SUPERIORITY: remember that you and I are not the heroes in this story. We have the Egyptian impulse to segregate in ourselves. Dennis Prager pointed out that every single group in history thought of itself as in some way exalted or special over others. This is not unique to Egyptians. It is built in to the human mind and how it relates to the world.
- WHY WE SEGREGATE: It is **overwhelming to consider that everyone in this room is as complex an individual as ourselves,** with dreams and fears and feelings of self-worth. When we see someone from our group, there is a sense of familiarity, of “expected-ness”. When we see people not in our group, there is an “unknown-ness”, a distrust. They subconsciously go into the “stranger” section. “the” Jews, “the” Germans, “the” Presbyterians, “the” whites. Within your own group, people are individuals and have names. But when you break out “the”, then they lose their names. They become objects in the group more than human individuals.
- NECESSARY FOR FORGING A PEOPLE: as pointed out by David Guzik, God took them out of Canaan where all the peoples mixed, and into Egypt where, as shepherds, they would be segregated, even under Joseph. Had they stayed in Canaan, they would have become Canaanites. Just look at Judah’s story. First real trouble and he starts marrying Canaanites and sleeping with Canaanite prostitutes. - JOSEPH WAS ALREADY MOSTLY EGYPTIAN: you see how difficult it is for Jews to remain Jews when the society around them accepts them. Joseph himself, one of Israel’s 12 original sons, was already basically fully Egyptian. He dresses like them, he married them, he speaks their language. There was nothing Hebrew about his life. First generation, already assimilated.
- WHAT DID BENJAMIN THINK? Why was he getting so much more food than everyone else? Did the brothers think? “oh here we go again, Benjamin the favorite.” Joseph could have been testing them to see if they still took the path of Cain when they saw Benjamin’s blessing.
- PARABLE OF THE WORKERS: Jesus said the kingdom of heaven is like this dinner in this way of choosing to celebrate or to be jealous of the blessing of others. In the end, this story is about the Path of Cain: how do you react to the undeserved blessing of others? Do you resent the deathbed conversion? Ways that Joseph is a type of Christ.: fractals on butterfly? Natural log zooming?

- Jesus’ human brothers, the Jews, plotted to kill him, thinking to keep their power and position with Rome. They thought he was dead, then miraculously they find him alive and ruling over everyone.
- Spiritual brothers (sons of God) threw him into the pit expecting him to die or be defeated there, but God raised him up in a way they did not expect and put him over them
- Jesus was taken to Egypt by God as a child to escape being murdered by the King of Israel because of a prophecy that he would be king over Israel, and Joseph was take to Egypt by God as a young man to escape being murdered by the sons of Israel because of dreams that prophesied he would be king over Israel.
- Joseph began gathering in the harvest in his primary position at age 30 in preparation for the seven years of famine and suffering. Jesus began his public ministry gathering the harvest of souls at age 30 in preparation for the final seven years of famine and suffering.
- Joseph, like Jesus, is numbered with the transgressors though he had done nothing wrong. Two transgressors were with Jesus, one saved with him and one perished. Just like the Cup Bearer and the Baker with Joseph.
- Pharoah says of Jacob how can we find a man like this in whom is the spirit of God, just as the Pharisee asks who can forgive sins but God alone, or the disciples ask who is this man that even the wind and the seas obey him?
- Joseph was punished without complaint for the sins of others: his brother’s, Potiphar’s wife’s sins
- Joseph was sold for 20 shekels of silver, Jesus for 30 pieces of silver
- Joseph fed thousands with miraculous bread. Jesus fed thousands with miraculous bread.
- Joseph did not use his high position to make others serve him, instead he used it to serve others. Jesus said that he came not to be served, but to serve.
- Mark 6:4: And Jesus said to them, “A prophet is not without honor, except in his hometown and among his relatives and in his own household.” And he could do no mighty work there, except that he laid his hands on a few sick people and healed them. And he marveled because of their unbelief.
- And most of all, Joseph is a singular messianic savior prototype. A lone figure standing against the storm, only able to stand because of his relationship with God. The world who rejected and punished Joseph is the very world that Joseph alone saves. Now imagine the brothers coming back to Joseph and refusing his gift. Joseph says, “You rejected me, but I declare to you I am your brother and you are my brothers. By my suffering God has made a way to save you from this judgment. Just admit your guilt, repent of your former sins, trust me and trust in God’s plan and accept this food from me for free by grace. You did not earn this salvation, but all you have to do is accept it and move here.” What if the brothers said “No, I don’t want your food. I’m not a sinner, you deserved what you got. I will not accept your free food.” And Joseph said “But God’s judgment is 6 more years of famine. There is no other food. You will die. Why not just accept the free gift? Why choose to go back to the outer darkness and perish instead of just accept a free gift?”
