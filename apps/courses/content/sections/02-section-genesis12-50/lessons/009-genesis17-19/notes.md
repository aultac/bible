**Genesis 17:1-8 > Covenant**

- EL SHADDAI: God almighty = El Shaddai
- WAITING: Imagine Abram’s 24 years of waiting (75->99), he keeps telling Sarai that God said they will have children. Every day. For 25 years. 75->86 Ismael, then 86->100 Isaac
- NAME CHANGE: Abram means “exalted father, great man”. Abraham means “father of many”. Imagine telling his name to visitors or long-time friends (Mamre, for example).
- ABRAHAMIC COVENANT: began in Genesis 12:4 - unconditional, people and property
- DAVIDIC COVENANT: 1 Chronicles 17:10 - Solomon and Jesus
- NEW COVENANT: EZEKIEL 16:48-50,62-63Sodom’s main sin in this story was cruelty to strangers. Ezekiel 16:49-53? The Hebrew word for “abomination” is the word used for male homosexuality in the Old Testament. Cover Sodom’s additional sins, point out that **Abraham’s descendants are worse than Sodom**, and God’s promise to atone for them.
- REVOLUTIONARY DEPENDABILITY: In all other cultures and religions the gods acted capriciously. Prager: “When God covenanted with Noah and Abram, He pledged to be dependable and trustworthy, thereby creating for the first time immutable spiritual and moral laws.”
- ABRAHAM WAS SO LUCKY:. God just chose him and made all these promises. If only he would choose me too and make promises. HE HAS! All you have to do is **trust the evidence he gave you that he will do what he says.**

**Genesis 17:8-14 > Circumcision**

- NOT NEW: Circumcision was a practice in Egypt 1000 years before Abraham
- CIRCUMCISION vs BAPTISM: Circumcision to a Jew is what baptism is to a Christian. It makes you part of the group.
- REMOVAL OF THE FLESH: Circumcision is a removal of flesh, a willingness to cut away the flesh for covenant with God
- CUT OFF: Punishment for not circumcising us to be cut off from his people: not clear what this means

**Genesis 17:15-end > all circumcised**

- INTIMACY: ISRAEL VS ISLAM: Look at the intimacy with God: Abraham was comfortable laughing with him, respectfully questioning him, asking him for evidence…contrast Israel (struggle with God) with Islam (submission to God). The God of Islam allows no relationship. The real God expects struggle and relationship.
- BOTH LAUGHED: Both Sarai AND Abraham laughed.
- ISHMAEL AND MUSLIMS: Ishmael is 13 when Abraham is 99. Ishmael is circumcised at 13, which is why Muslim boys are circumcised at 13 today.
- CIRCUMCISION WITHOUT COVENANT: None of the people circumcised except Sarah and Abraham are actually part of the covenant.

**Genesis 18:1-8 > Three men meet Abraham**

- APPEARANCE OF YAHWEH: CHRISTOPHANY: how can Yahweh appear to Abraham as a man? Angel of Yahweh! Jesus! Jewish commentator I love, Dennis Prager, was perplexed by this. His only recourse was that Yahweh spoke through one of the three strangers. But that’s not what it says.
- HOSPITALITY: Must understand the importance of hospitality in the Middle East and in the ancient near eastern cultures. Just look at lot: he considered it better to have his daughter’s raped than to allow a stranger who was due his hospitality to be harmed.
- MILK AND MEAT: Abram served milk and meat. Jews don’t eat milk and meat because of “**fence around the law**” to not boil a kid in its mother’s milk Deuteronomy 14:21. Testament to the age and truthfulness of the text: later author would not have set such a story.

**Genesis 18:9-15 > Sarah laughs**

- CHRISTOPHANY: Now we learn Yahweh is there. How can Abram see him and live? Angel of Yahweh, pre-incarnate Christ
- SARAI: Remember that both Abram and Sarai laughed. Criterion of Embarrassment: Sarai even lied to God! God had mercy on her.

**Genesis 18:16-end > Abram argues with God**

- ONE LAST CHANCE: God’s angels appear as their final test (“I will go down to see…”), and they treat them just as bad as everyone else.
- WAY OF THE LORD: God tells us that the “way of the LORD” is “doing righteousness and justice”. Abram was justified by his faith, but he was called to do righteousness and justice.
- ASSUME GOD IS JUST?: Why did Abraham assume that God is just? There’s no other instance prior to this of man challenging a deity on moral grounds. No other humans assumed their gods were just. Gods were capricious. It is completely revolutionary not only for Abram to argue with God, but to argue on the basis of morality.
- REVOLUTIONARY: Revolutionary to argue for saving people because they are righteous.
- POWER = RIGHT: The pagan worldview defines strength as justice, power as righteous. From “Roman Helmet Guy” on X: “The worst thing about Nolan’s Odyssey hasn’t even been discussed yet. - HOMER vs. GOD: Where Homer celebrates the cunning warrior, Christianity elevates the merciful. The best Christian leaders are the best servants, the best non-Christian leaders are the most powerful.
- ABRAM ARGUES FOR SODOM: they are not his people, his ethnicity, his race, or his religion. Abraham’s protest establishes that **divine justice must distinguish between categories of people based on their moral character**.
- HISTORY HATES JEWS FOR GOD’S MORALITY: As quoted by the Jewish commentator Dennis Prager, a Roman Catholic scholar, father Edward Flannery, “**It was Judaism who brought the concept of a God-given universal moral law into the world, and, willingly or not, the Jew carries the burden of God in history and for this has never been forgiven.**”

But there is a difference between “all sin separates us from God” and “sin so bad that I will destroy the cities with fire from heaven.” **There seem here to indeed be better and worse sins**: God did not destroy the other cities, only these. Jesus speaks of how it will be worse on the day of judgement for those who saw him than for Sodom and Gomorrah.
