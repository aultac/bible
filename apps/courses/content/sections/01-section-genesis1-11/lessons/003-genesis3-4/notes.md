## Genesis 3 - 4 mins reading + 7 mins other reading + 11:30 comments

**Genesis 3:1 only**

- **NACHASH**: Every good story has a villain and ours is about to show up
- Verse 1: Nachash is unlike what we know as a serpent: use the word Nachash “Shining One”. This is more like a dragon (Revelation 20). There is overlap with a physical snake, but Satan is a spiritual being
- any “other” beast: other isn’t in the Hebrew. Since this thing talks, it seems like the Hebrew “was more cunning that any beast of the field” fits better. There is overlap with a physical snake, but Satan is a spiritual being.
- Think of how a snake looks like it is covered in emeralds, topaz, etc. “Shining” in the grass.
- Nachash in Ezekiel 28, Isaiah 14. **4 mins total reading**
- He is “the accuser”: he spends history getting humans to sin and accusing them before God to show God how he is so much better than these weak, awful humans.
- Numbers 21:4-9. Note they end up worshiping that snake as Nehushtan 2 Kings 18:4. **1 min total reading**.

- **NAMES OF GOD**: Genesis 2 started using LORD God. Go through 2 names of God. Yahweh, Elohim, and Yahweh Elohim.
- Elohim is plural: The fish swims vs The fish swim. Mathematics is vs. mathematics are. What is Elohim vs what are Elohim? Psalm 82: “sons of God”, gods of the other nations, angels, demons, even Samuel’s ghost with the witch at Endor. Elohim (plural) = Spirit being with certain authority.
- Notice how Satan drops the more personal Yahweh and Eve repeats it

**Genesis 2-7 (up to loincloths)**

- **PLAYBOOK**: Satan’s playbook: 3 steps:
  1. Did God really say - doubt or twist word of God
  2. For God knows - call into question God’s motives or nature
  3. …and you will be like God…: root of all human evil is attempting to displace God as the arbiter of moral authority. You decide what is right and wrong. “I can’t believe in a God who would tell me who I can love…”
- Satan doesn’t push you to do evil. **He pushes you to do whatever you think is right.**
- **RIGHT IN MY OWN EYES**: FIRST Judges last verse 21:25, then Judges: 19:22-30: Crime at Gibeah. Israel and Benjamin go to war. Israel kills everyone except 600 that escape. They kill all the women and children. Then they worry that those 600 won’t have wives because they vowed not to give them their daughters. So they kill everybody in Jabesh Gilead and steal 400 virgins. Need 200 more, so they tell Benjamin to wait in ambush for the daughters of Shiloh to come to the festival at Jerusalem and just kidnap them. Judges 21:25: “In those days there was no king in Israel. Everyone did what was right in his own eyes.” This is what it means for man to become like God, determining Good and Evil for himself. **2 mins reading**
- See why it is so important for us to be loading this up into our heads? If we don’t know God‘s word, then we are worse off than Eve in the story. Imagine if she didn’t even know that God had told them not to eat the fruit?

## Chapter 4 - 3:30 mins reading + 1 min other reading + 6:30 commentary

**Genesis 4:1-7 (to you must rule over it):**

- It doesn’t tell us how Cain knew that God had no regard for his offering.
- **SACRIFICES**: SKIP IF SHORT ON TIME! Malachi chapter 1:6: God’s commentary on inadequate sacrifices

**Genesis 8-10 (to “What have you done?”)**

- **PERPETRATORS VS VICTIMS**: Am I my brother’s keeper? Contrast Cain’s response to God to Adam’s response asking where he was in the garden. Adam and Eve hid themselves. They were ashamed. Cain just blows it off like it’s not his problem.
- When you read history like this, your tendency is to read it as a victim, but that is not accurate: you are far more likely to be the Perpetrator. Cain’s reaction is there in each of us. Without Christ, we would most likely all have had Cain’s reaction in the same situation. Sin is crouching at your door.
- **REACTION TO EXCELLENCE**: When someone excels you, you have the choice to **hate them, one-up them, emulate them, or honor them**. Graph of this vs. how well you know them, but curve flattens as you age. Cain chose hate. We all want to be honored by our fellow human beings, but that is actually the last thing that any of us tends to give to others.

**Genesis 10-11 (until ground soaks up blood):**

- **BLOODS**: Your brother‘s bloods plural cry out to me from the ground. Jewish tradition says the plural indicates all the people who would never be born. The entire nation of evil did not get a chance to live. That’s how bad murder is. Maybe Abel’s descendants would have cured cancer a long time ago.
- First clue we have that the ground soaks up sin.
- Hebrews 12:18-24: Jesus blood speaks a better word than Abel’s blood. Jesus did not cry out for vengeance which was his right, instead he cried out for forgiveness of his captors.
