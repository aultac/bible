- GOD CHOOSES TO REMEMBER: The difference between God’s remembering and your remembering js God’s remembering is a choice. You are a slave to your forgetfulness. The Bible says God remembers when he is choosing to act.

- PLEASING AROMA: Contrast God smelling the pleasing aroma of the sacrifices with the other Mesopotamian flood stories where the gods had been deprived of human sacrifices, and they swarmed them like flies after the flood was over. Those gods were dependent on humans. This God is completely different than any God they knew. A big part of the Torah’s purpose is to introduce humanity to the God completely unlike any other.
- ANTHROPOMORPHIZING GOD: Anthropomorphizing God: The text makes it sound like God said he would never destroy the world again because of the pleasing aroma of the sacrifice. God does not have a nose, he does not have an appetite. **The God of the Torah was different from all other Gods: he does not eat.** The “showbread” in the tabernacle is a daily reminder that God does not eat. The other Gods needed the humans to sacrifice after the flood because they were dependent on the humans to provide their food.
- GOD CHOOSES TO SMELL: Just like God “chooses” to remember, he “chooses” to regret making man and God “chooses”: to smell the pleasing aroma: we want him to, and if he accepts our sacrifice, then he will choose to smell the pleasing aroma because that is what we are asking him to do. Don’t lame and crippled animals smell the same as unblemished ones?

**Genesis 11:1-9**: 1:30

- ZIGGURATS APP: Connect heaven and earth.
- FAME IS DESTRUCTIVE: Self-destructive quest for fame: Make a name for ourselves, or bring glory to ourselves. Instead of God. The least happy people are famous people.
- THE MADE A NAME FOR THEMSELVES: They wanted to make a name for themselves, and in the end they did, just not the sort of name they intended. Thanks to the Torah of the God that they threw off, their name has forever, been associated with confusion. Babel because the languages sound like “babble”
- COME DOWN: Note the joke that God had to “come down” to even see the supposedly amazing tower and city.
- “US” AGAIN: Who is “us”? Sons of God, same “us” as in Genesis 1. “You get a nation, you get a nation, …”
- ANYONE CAN CHOOSE GOD: The people of Babel did not want God to be their God. God appointed his sons over the nations, but left open through Jesus that anyone in any other nation under any other god can choose to follow the Most High god instead. That is fundamentally what it’s all about: choosing to follow God. That is what faith is all about. And that is why it has always been about faith.

- WORLD UNITY IS NOT GOOD: God could have just toppled the tower, but that did not solve the underlying problem. The underlying problem is that too many people of the same mind will pool their resources to their own destruction and the destruction of those around them. As the Internet returns the world to a common ability to communicate, it is entirely reasonable to think this might be a step on the way to the book of Revelation.
- NATIONALISM IS BETTER: God tells us that world unity is not a good idea. This is in contrast to the secular communist, ideal that we are all citizens of the world instead of citizens of our respective nations. It is in fact, a significantly better scenario to have nations and each nation is proud of its own heritage.
- UNITY IS DISINGENUOUS: Calls for unity are always disingenuous. What they mean is unity under my values. No Protestant who calls for Christian unity says I’m perfectly happy to accept the Pope. Unity as an end in itself is purely a pretext for me controlling you.
- CITIES ARE BAD: God is not a fan of cities. The higher the population density, the more anonymous you are. And the more anonymous you are the cruel you are. Mob-rule, groupthink, man starts to focus on controlling those around him, first for defense, but inevitably progressing to social engineering on the way to totalitarianism

- GOD’S WILL IS ALWAYS ACCOMPLISHED: They disobeyed God (multiply and fill the earth). In the end, God’s will to fill the Earth was accomplished. We can do this the hard way or the easy way, but it is going to happen.
