SUPREME GOD
Thebes • Karnak

Amun-Ra

Animal Form: Ram

Primary sacred animal of Amun-Ra; ram-headed sphinxes lined the processional ways at Karnak.

Sacrificing a ram or lamb was viewed as a direct attack on the visible form of Egypt’s supreme national deity during the 18th Dynasty.

Hathor

Animal Form: Cow

Cow-headed or woman with cow horns and sun disk — goddess of joy and the afterlife.

Explicit 18th-Dynasty cow form. Cows were generally protected as sacred to Hathor/Isis, making any related sacrifice provocative.

Ptah (Apis Bull)

Animal Form: Bull

Living Apis bull as the sacred manifestation of Ptah in Memphis.

Direct link to Hebrew sacrifice: The Hebrews’ offering of bulls and calves targeted the sacred living embodiment of the creator god Ptah — one of the most explicit “abominations” possible.

Ra-Horakhty

Animal Form: Falcon

Falcon-headed man with sun disk — the rising sun and cosmic order.

One of the most common depictions of the sun god. Hebrew burnt offerings stood in stark contrast to Ra’s daily solar journey.

Falcon-headed or full falcon — god of kingship and the sky.

This 18th-Dynasty statue (Amenhotep II era) shows the falcon form directly tied to pharaonic legitimacy — the very kingship the plagues challenged.

Iconic in New Kingdom tomb art. Burning animals in sacrifice would have contrasted sharply with Anubis’s role in preserving the body for eternity.

Animal Form: Lioness
