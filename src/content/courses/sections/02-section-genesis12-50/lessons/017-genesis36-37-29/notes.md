**Genesis 36:1-end > Esau’s Genealogy**

- EDOM RED: picture of Petra and red rocks, red hair, reminder of the red soup
- UNIVERSAL GOD: an entire chapter of Genesis is devoted to the line of non-Jewish peoples.
- AMELEK
  - Exodus 17: **Red Sea Attack**: Attack right after Red Sea.
  - Deuteronomy 25: **God mad at Amalekites**: , orders future Amalekite destruction.
  - Numbers 14: **Amalekites beat rogue Israelites**: rogue Israelites enter Canaan without God after spies report, Amalekites beat them.
  - Judges 3: **Amalek + Moab -> Ehud**: Judge Ehud stabs fat Moabite King Eglon and locks the door
  - Judges 6: **Amelek + Midian-> Gideon**: Judge Gideon beats them.
  - 1 Samuel 15: **Saul spares Agag**: God orders Saul to wipe out Amelekites, Saul spares Agag. Tradition says he fathered a child day before being killed.
  - 1 Samuel 27: **David raids Amalek from Ziklag**: David secretly raids Amalekites from Ziklag working for Philistine king Achish
  - 1 Samuel 30: **Amalekites burn Ziklag**: and steal families, David and his men catch and crush them.
  - 2 Samuel 1: **Amalekite report of Saul’s death**: brings crown of Saul to David, claims to have killed him, David kills him.
  - 1 Chronicles 4: **Hezekiah “utterly destroyed” Amalekites**
  - Esther 3: **Haman the Agagite**: the first Hitler, “Haman”, is Agagite, descended from Agag because Saul did not do what God commanded.
- EDOM
  - Numbers 20: **Edom blocks Israel**: Edom denies Israel passage to promised land.
  - Deuteronomy 2 & 23: **Don’t detest an Edomite**: they are your brothers.
  - 2 Samuel 8: **David rules Edom**
  - 1 Kings 11: **Hadad rebels against Solomon**: the Edomite rebels against Solomon, southern and eastern Edom independent.
  - 2 Kings 8: **West Edom rebels -> Jehoram**.
  - 2 Kings 14: **Amaziah takes Petra**: MAP!
  - Obadiah: **Book of Edom’s destruction**: Entire book is prophecy of Edom’s destruction for gloating over Babylon.
  - 500-400BC: **Nabateans ->** **Hebron**: Nabatean Arabs chase Edom into Hebron during Persian empire, Nabateans carve rocks at Petra.
  - 300BC: **Hellenization -> Idumea**: Greek empire, Ptolemies (General Ptolemy) followed Alexander the Great
  - 200BC: **Seleucids defeat Ptolemies**: descendants of Seleucus general under Alexander
  - 167BC: **Maccabees (Hasmoneans) rebel:** against Seleucid kings after Anitiochus Epiphanies IV for desecrating the temple with pig sacrifice and an altar to Zeus. Hasmoneans rule for a little while.
  - 110BC: **John Hyrcanus forces conversion**: Hasmonean King/Priest John Hycanus forcibly converts and circumcizes all Idumeans.
  - 63BC: **Pharisees vs. Sadducees -> Rome wins with Pompey**: Pharisees back Hasmonean Hyrcanus II, Sadducees back his brother Aristobulus II in civil war. Pompey conquers Judah for Rome, demotes Hyrcanus II to only high priest
  - 37BC: **Idumean Herod the Great**: marries into Hasmonean family for legitimacy: Jewish and Idumean. Herod is called “the great” because he built lots of projects, **invented pouring concrete underwater** to build Caesarea as man-made deep water port to compete with Tyre.
  - 20BC: **Herod expands temple**: still under construction 46 years later in John 2:20.
  - 2AD: **Baby killing**: Herod the Great tries to kill Jesus as a baby: murders everyone under 2.
  - There are 7 Herod’s, all Idumean:
    - 37BC-4AD: **Herod the Great** \- murdered babies to try to get Jesus, kingdom splits among 3 sons
    - 4BC - 4AD: **Herod Archelaus:** west, brutal, deposed, led to direct Roman prefect-rule like Pontius Pilate.
    - 4BC - 39AD: **Herod Antipas: east (Galilee) beheads John the Baptist**
    - 4BC - 34AD: **Herod Philip:** northeast, also “Phillip the Tetrarch”
    - 37AD - 44AD: **Herod Agrippa I**: kills James and imprisons Peter
    - 41 AD - 48AD: **Herod Chalcis**:rules Jerusalem after his brother Agrippa I dies.
    - 50AD - 93AD: **Herod Agrippa II:** Paul preaches to him in Acts 25

**Genesis 37:29-end > bloody robe**

- SONS DON’T LIE: Jacob draws all the conclusions
- “YOUR SON’S TUNIC”: needlessly cruel
- JACOB DECEIVED BY GOAT: just as he deceived his father by feeding him a goat and using goat skin on his arms, so he is deceived by a goat into thinking Joseph is dead.
- COMFORTER BY DAUGHTERS: could be general term for daughters in law, or son’s daughters, or unnamed actual daughters
- MIDIANITES SOLD HIM IN EGYPT: the actual word is “towards” Egypt, not “in Egypt”, as in they sold him to the Ishmaelites who were headed toward Egypt and to Potiphar. Otherwise it is a contradiction with the earlier verse that the Ishmaelites took him to Egypt. I don’t think the brothers actually know what happened to Joseph.

**Genesis 38:1-11 > Tamar widowed**

- JUDAH LEFT HIS BROTHERS: it was his idea to sell Jacob, for better or worse, maybe that’s why he left
- PARALLEL TIME WITH JOSEPH: Parallel with Joseph story:
  - Joseph 11 years with Potiphar, 2 prison, 7 years plenty = 20 years
  - Judah 5 years boys born, Er and Onan die around 15 (10 years later), Shelah grows to 15-ish, so right at 20 years, right before heading to Egypt.
- MARRYING CANAANITES FORBIDDEN: in Deuteronomy. Judah does it, further proof of antiquity of Torah.
- LEVIRATE MARRIAGE: Levir is Latin for “brother-in-law”. Deuteronomy 25:5-10 lets the spurned wife of the dead man spit in the brother’s face and remove his sandal. It is seen as a solemn duty of the brother, not a burden forced on the woman, as we’ll see with Tamar.
- ONAN’S SIN: his sin is not “wasting his seed” to keep it PG: his sin is intentionally cheating Tamar and his brother out of children, taking what he wants from her and not giving her what she wanted.
- ONAN WON’T GIVE BROTHER AN HEIR: his brother Er was evil after all, maybe that played a factor. Or maybe Onan was just a jerk, or Onan did not want to split the inheritance.
- BROTHERS IN GENESIS STINK: Ishmael makes fun of Isaac. Jacob steals Esau’s blessing. Rebekah’s brother Laban takes advantage of her son Jacob. Onan refuses Er an heir. Joseph’s brothers hate him and plot his murder. Maybe I’m glad I didn’t have a brother. The only brother I would like to have from Genesis is Esau.
- FIRST INDIVIDUAL DEATHS FOR EVIL: no one before this specifically named and individually killed. All others were corporate punishment. The contrast is more striking given that these were sons of the patriarch who had special blessing.

**Genesis 38:12-23 > Judah sleeps with Tamar**

- JUDAH WITHOLDS SHELAH: he is scared to lose a third son, thinking Tamar might have been the common denominator. He clearly never intended to give her to Shelah.
- STILL WEARING WIDOW’S CLOTHES: a girl “sent home” is in disgrace.
- SEAL AND STAFF: driver’s license and credit cards. It’s an odd request: what could she even do with these? They are valuable to Judah, but not likely really usable by Tamar.
- WHAT TOOK YOU SO LONG? I stopped at church.
- CULT PROSTITUTE VS HARLOT: cult prostitute seems more respectable than harlot. Unclear if the Adullamite was protecting Judah’s reputation (though he was unmarried at the time), or if Judah lied to him. **Every pagan god was worshipped through sex**.
- KEDESHA MEANS HOLY WOMAN: The word for “cult prostitute” here is “kedesha”, which literally means “holy woman.” We would call her literally the opposite, an “unholy woman”. Imagine men thinking that it was not only not wrong to lie with this woman, but doing so was a holy act desired by God! **Just one more reason wives had hard lives outside the Torah-based world.** “Sorry, honey, I don’t want to do this but the crops won’t grow and we’ll starve if I don’t….”
- SEX VS SACRIFICE: Women did their religious duty as “holy women” prostitutes, and men did their duty by frequenting them. This is why the Israelites keep falling away from God: **between the God who you worship by admitting your guilt and offering sacrifice to prevent your punishment, and the God who you show devotion to through one night stands with scores of “holy women,” which do you think is going to win out?** Without the law of God and Christ in our hearts, do you think most men would rather go to a strip club or to church?
- DESEXUALIZATION OF RELIGION: we do not appreciate this in the modern west, but it may very well be that the single greatest innovation of the Torah was the complete desexualization of religion. Nearly every non-Abrahamic religion in history has been highly sexualized. **It was the appearance of this book on the world stage that is the reason you today do not equate church with sex.** The idea that “church” is not sex with prostitutes is by far the oddity in human history, not the other way around.

**Genesis 38:24-end > Perez is born**

- BURNING ALIVE: this is not standard practice for adultery anywhere else in the Bible. And Judah shows no self-awareness of how he was withholding Shelah. Perhaps he just saw it as an easy end to his problem.
- TAMAR DOES NOT PUBLICLY REBUKE JUDAH: even though he was going to burn her alive, she sends him his stuff privately.
- JUDAH TRANSFORMED: that one moment seeing the seal and cord transformed Judah’s entire life. Incredibly, instead of denying it or trying to put her to death anyway, he 100% admits his guilt in its entirety. Most people do not do this. There were no excuses from Judah. Right after this, he takes full responsibility for Benjamin and offers his life for Benjamin’s. **No one prior to Judah has ever admitted fault. Remember this fact, it will be important!**

**Genesis 39:1-6 > Joseph’s first slave job**

- JOSEPH PATTERNS ISRAEL: we will see how Joseph is a type of Christ, but you see here how he is also a mirror of the eventual history of the Jewish people. He rises to prominence, that is attacked and in prison, then he rises to prominence, then is attacked and brought down, then rises again once freed. This can be attributed to both God choosing them, and the culture God gave them: nearly universal marriage, strong families, universal literacy among both sexes, and a focus on academic study.
- DEPICTING GOOD EGYPTIANS: despite being written by Moses, after escaping 400 years of slavery in Egypt, the Torah often depicts Egyptian characters in a positive light. Potiphar is kind to Joseph, it is his wife that is a problem.
- BLESSED THE HOUSE BECAUSE OF JOSEPH: just as Laban’s house was blessed because of Jacob. At this point in the story, if you didn’t know the rest of the Bible, you would 100% expect that God’s promise has now flowed through Joseph uniquely of all the brothers just as it did to Jacob instead of Esau. The only hint you have that might not be the case is Perez coming out before his brother has. Jacob grabbed the heel of his brother. But since we know the rest, **we see that God‘s promise actually flows through the first person in the Bible to have contrition, to admit they were a sinner, Judah.**

**Genesis 39:7-end > Joseph imprisoned**

- GENDER ROLES REVERSED: Prager pointed out that one of the odd things about this story is that the typical gender roles are reversed: the woman is in the position of power and demanding sex, and the man is subordinate, good looking, and refuses
- NO DIRECT CONTACT WITH GOD: unlike the three patriarchs before Joseph, Joseph never had direct conversation with God that we know of. And yet he invoke God as the reason he will not sleep with Potiphar’s wife. He always keeps God central in his reasoning, giving God, the credit for his ability to interpret dreams and his success.
