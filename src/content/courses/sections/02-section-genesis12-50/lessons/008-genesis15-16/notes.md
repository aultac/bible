**Genesis 15:1-6**

- DO NOT FEAR: The first thing the word of the Lord says to him is fear not. God is terrifying in person. **The most frequent statement of God to man in the Bible is do not fear.**
- TERROR VS. REVERENCE: Wisdom begins with fear of God. We are to revere God fearfully, but he does not want his presence to terrify us beyond our ability to listen. He loves us and does not want the terror, but he demands the reverence.
- BIBLICAL FAITH: Biblical faith is trusting that God will do what he said. It is NOT “faith that he exists.” When I say “____, I have faith in you”, I am not telling you that I believe you exist. I am telling you that I trust you based on the evidence of your past behavior and who you are. The Bible treats God’s existence as a given because creation has a creator like a painting has a painter, it says we have no excuse for not knowing that he exists.
- NOT BLIND FAITH: **God does NOT ask for blind faith**. God does NOT respond to Abram “Stop whining about evidence and just have blind faith.” He gives evidence all the time in the Bible. He says that is why we have miracles, which is why we have a regular universe. It’s WHY Jesus was resurrected. For Abram, he:
  - talks directly to him (evidence #1),
  - he afflicts Pharaoh (evidence #2) ,
  - he defeats the kings (evidence #3),
  - he performs a covenant ritual (evidence #4),
  - Tells him multiple times to reassure him (evidence #5),
  - Gets Hagar to return (evidence #6),
  - Appears to him in person, eats with him, and calls out Sarah for laughing about his promise (evidence #7)
- NOT ONLY FAITH HERE: we need to be honest and recognize that this does not say that we are ONLY saved by faith, or that the ONLY righteousness there is comes by faith. It just says that trusting God IS righteous because of who God is. There are other righteous things.
- BUT ROMANS 4: Does this teach that there is no righteousness other than faith like Abram? No, it teaches that we are only JUSTIFIED by faith before God.
  - Romans 4:1-4: If we were justified by following the law, then God “owes” us wages, God has “debt” to us.
  - Romans 4:5-8: But we all sin according to the law. David doesn’t say “blessed is the man who never sinned.” He says blessed is the man whose sins are forgiven.
  - Romans 4:9-12: This “faith is counted as righteousness” setup is started with Abraham, making him the father to everyone who trusts God, not only Israelites
  - Romans 4:13-16:
    - God’s promise to Abram was unconditional: it did not depend on Abram doing righteous things and earning it. Only God can be trusted. Otherwise we would not have reason to trust this particular promise. We can only trust because it did not depend on man.
    - But the law only brings wrath: you break it, you buy it. Good deeds do not erase bad deeds. Only blood can pay.
    - You cannot trust the promise of your own goodness, it will fail you. You can only trust the promise of God as a free gift, not dependent on your own righteousness.
    - “Faith is null and the promise is void”: law only brings wrath, so the promise would be meaningless or “void” if based on the law since we would have **EARNED** it instead of being given it as a promised gift. The only other avoidance of wrath would be to have **no law at all**, which makes faith pointless **for justification** since right and wrong would not exist.
  - Romans 4:17-22: TRUST IN SUCH A HOLY BEING = RIGHTEOUSNESS: God is so Holy and Good, that NOT trusting him is bad/evil, and trusting him IS righteousness. How Holy and Good? **CHART my goodness+holiness vs God**
    - SIN AGAINST GOD WORTHY OF HELL: If you sin against him you deserve Hell. Tearing leaves off a flower -> wings and legs off a fly -> legs off a puppy -> legs off a baby. Understand just how Holy and Good God is: sinning against that is deserving of Hell. Trusting in that is righteousness.
  - Romans 4:23-5:1: Abram is the father of all who trust God.
- THIS IS CRITICAL: this one line about faith counted as righteousness sets the foundation for the ENTIRE plan of salvation. Paul says it’s the ONLY plan that could ever have worked! We will be justified before God by believing that he will do what he said, not by earning it.

**Genesis 15:7-end**

- WHY TELL ABRAM?: why did God tell Abram what would happen to his descendants? What use was that to Abram? It may have been told him solely for the benefit of those people in Egypt.
- GENERATION=LIFETIME: At Abram’s time, “generation” originally meant “lifetime”: about 100 years each
- WHY SLAVES?: Torah does not say why they have to be slaves and not just live happily. But when Jews live in a country not their own, people always come to suspect and hate them. Ever since God chose them, Satan specifically targets them for hatred. The easiest hatred in the history of the world is anti-Semitism because Satan will encourage it within you every chance he gets. Since Canaan was the homeland, and they couldn’t go there yet, they were going to be slaves wherever they lived.
- AMMORITES NOT MAXIMUM EVIL: this is why we are still here as well, Revelation does not end the Earth until everyone has been saved that could be saved.
- RITUAL: The animal ritual was a classic near Eastern covenant-sealing blood ceremony. The one who passes through the middle is saying that he deserves the fate of the animals if he breaks the covenant. Normally both parties pass through the middle, but only God’s fire passes through the middle showing a unilateral contract.
