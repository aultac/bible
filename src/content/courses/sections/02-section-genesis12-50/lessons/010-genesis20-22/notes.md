- MIDRASH IS FILLING IN THE BLANKS: This is a week of more questions than answers. And everybody wants to pile on Abraham. Maybe we should give Abraham the benefit of the doubt: maybe he was right!

**Genesis 20:1-end > Abimelech**

- MAP: between Kadesh and Shur in Gerar
- TAKE AN OLD WOMAN?: Sarah is 89 years old. Why did Abimelech take her?
- ABRAHAM’S ARMY: Abraham had an army of at least 300 men who defeated the 4 kings. Why does he not fight instead of lying?
- DREAM OF DREAD: Imagine God telling you that you are a dead man
- INNOCENT HANDS: Notice Sarah had no say in Abimelech’s “innocent hands” taking her. But this was the norm.
- SIN AGAINST GOD: God says the sin was against him, not Abraham.
- PROPHET: First mention of “prophet “ in the Bible. Prophet means spokesperson for God, not “teller of the future”.
- GOD-GIVEN AUTHORITY: God tells Abimelech then basically he will not forgive him, unless Abraham forgives him. Abimelech must humble himself before Abraham solely because of Abraham’s position as prophet to the Most High God of the universe.
- NO FEAR OF GOD: Abraham’s primary accusation against Abimelech is “no fear of God.” The only restraint on humans who are either in charge (king) or who think they won’t get caught is if they think God is watching. Notice Abimelech does not object to this accusation. Maybe Abraham was right!
- OLD, TRUTHFUL TEXT: Leviticus 18:9-11 and others prohibit marrying a half-sister. A later Jewish writer would not make up the patriarch marrying a half-sister.

- FINALLY

**Genesis 21:8-21 > Ishmael cast out**

- DANGER OF FAVORITES AMONG CHILDREN: Ishmael is 14, Isaac is the favored child of the promise. He is the little prince. Foreshadowing of Joseph and of Jesus.
- ABRAHAM LOVED ISHMAEL: He was his only son for 14 years. The man whose life’s promise was to have a son. He begged God to help him. He taught him everything he knew: Hunting, fishing, worshipping God, leadership, morality, everything. Now he is just turning 17. You wonder why he became a wild donkey of a man, when his Dad excommunicated him at 17 because he was acting like a normal brother.
- ROSE EARLY IN THE MORNING: this means it was important in the Bible
- HEARTBREAKING: Imagine the actual moment Abraham had to break the news to Hagar and Ishmael.
- RIGHT IDEA WRONG REASON: Why Abraham did not give Ishmael any animals or servants or any inheritance at all that would normally be due to a firstborn son? The only thing that makes sense to that he has to completely avoid any chance that hundreds of years later some of Ishmael’s descendants demand a share. Sarah’s idea was motivated by jealousy, but God still affirmed it as right in the end.
- SACRIFICE OF ISHMAEL: Abraham had to basically sacrifice his first son Ishmael, trusting entirely that God would not let him die. Both sons were saved by a voice from heaven.
- PAUL’S TWO COVENANTS SLAVE (Torah) AND FREE (Christ): **Read Galatians 4:21-31.** Religious leaders wanted to keep the law, and Paul tells them their mother is Hagar. He is basically saying you want to focus on Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy as “the law”, but Genesis is in “the law” too and it says the child of the promise through whom all the families of the world will be blessed. That was the promise. The promise was not the law.
- MAP: Beersheba

**Genesis 21:22-end > promise Abimelech**

- LOTS OF SEVENS: seven ewes, seven Abimelech’s, seven Abraham’s (w/o verse 34), covenant is derived from “seven”, Beersheba is “well number seven” or “well of the covenant”

- CHANGED STATUS: see how Abraham now is no longer cowering in fear over being killed for Sarah’s beauty, but now a is a favored person Abimelech desires to keep around for good luck

**Genesis 22:1-8 > start trek to sacrifice Isaac**

- “HERE I AM”: hineni. Used by Abraham here, Esau to Isaac, Jacob to Isaac, Jacob to angel, Joseph to Israel, Moses to burning bush, Samuel to God, Isaiah to God “Here I am, send me”
- MORIAH: Map, then **Read 2 Samuel 24, then 2 Chronicles 3:1**: became threshing floor of Ornan (or Araunah: Ornan was a title like “Lord”) the Jebusite, bought by David to sacrifice, and eventually became temple.

**Genesis 22:9-14 > sacrifice Isaac**

- WHY PAINFUL ADJECTIVES?: Why did God rub it in by saying “your only son, the one that you love?” To add difficulty to the test.
- SAME COMMAND AS BEFORE: go out from your home to another land which I will show you
- WHY NOT ARGUE?: Why did Abraham argue for Sodom but not for Isaac? Differences:
  - God told him what God was going to do vs God commanded Abraham to do something.
  - Child sacrifice to your god was considered admirable in his culture, and was common. Children were viewed as property of the parents.
  - Abraham had a conflict of interest in Isaac such that he could not argue on the basis of the abstract moral principle of justice,
  - and Abraham had a direct promise from God that he trusted with Isaac and he already lost Ishmael, the result of trying to re-interpret God’s promise. God could raise the dead.
  - Sodom may very well have convinced Abraham that God was just because he never argued with God again in the text
- WHY DID **ISAAC** NOT ARGUE?: I realized that I was bringing my modern Western Christian view to this story. I assume the children were never willing sacrifices. But this was the norm. When a god asked for a sacrifice, you did it. Because he is a god and you are a human. It was not open for debate. Isaac may not in fact have blamed his dad at all.
- HINDSIGHT HELPS: Today we have the benefit of knowing God stayed Abraham’s hand. We would trust that we would to the same for us. Abraham had no such precedent.
- SPIRIT TELLS YOU THIS TODAY?: If this happened today, you question the spirit being on many grounds:
  - scripture shows a God who does not accept child sacrifice, but scripture did not exist for Abraham.
  - Jesus was the final sacrifice: God already provided a lamb for sacrifice: his OWN son, not yours.
  - The fact that this never happened again in history is why post-Abraham, this would only come from a demon.
- HOW OLD?: How old was Isaac? It doesn’t tell us. We know he was 37 when his mom died. One theory is this was shortly before that, so around 32 or 33. But Abraham had to bind him to put him on the wood, so he may have been much younger. Isaac was old enough to carry the wood, and Abraham let him presumably because he was old and in firm. So it may be that Isaac willingly participated. If so, he inherited his father‘s trust of God. If not, it must’ve been seriously dramatic for both of them
- FEEL THE STORY: Have you ever had to lead people to do something when you yourself weren’t sure what you should be doing? Imagine Abraham putting all this together: wood chop chop chop, servants, donkeys, map. Leave someone in charge while gone. Tell Sarah, don’t tell Sarah? Don’t forget the knife. The questions: where are you going? What are you doing? When will you be back? The expectant joy in Isaac’s face of a trip with Dad.
- DIRECT PURPOSE OF THE TEST: Dennis Prager put it, good people must be as committed to goodness as evil people are committed to evil. Abraham must be willing to sacrifice as much as the non-God-fearing people are to their gods.
- REVOLUTIONARY INDIRECT PURPOSE OF THE TEST: changed history. Taught the world that God did not want child sacrifice. It changed the relationship between fathers and sons forever. Israel still engaged in it with other gods, and God hated it. Why did only one people, one culture, one religion consider child sacrifice bad? Because of this teaching from God and the way he taught it. Because this God injected this into human history.
- UNUSUAL ANSWER: Why did Abraham answer Isaac in the way he did? Why did he say the Lord will provide for himself a sacrifice? The Lord gave Isaac to Abraham, the Lord has therefore provided the sacrifice. Foreshadowing Jesus!
- CHRIST PARALLELS:
  - Born miraculously
  - Both sons of the promise loved by their father
  - The “only” begotten son: other sons of God, like Ishmael, were different. This was the only son.
  - Both carried the wood
  - Jesus went willingly, presumably so did Isaac
  - Both like a lamb before the slaughter opened not his mouth
  - Both sacrificed on Mount Moriah
  - Hebrews 11 tells us that Abraham the father trusted even that God could raise the dead (both father’s knew this)
  - The lamb in the thicket took Isaac’s place, just as Jesus took ours.
  - A sacrifice was needed, the right sacrifice, and when God (Jesus) called down from heaven, he left it unfinished until he himself came.
- GOD IS MOST TRAGIC FIGURE: I have tried to help us feel the pain of Abraham in telling the story. The relief when God stopped him. But when God’s own son was there, nobody came. Nobody stopped it. The camera did it cut away.

**Genesis 22:15-end > Angel of Lord covenant**

- ANGEL OF YAHWEH: talks like Yahweh gave him a message, but earlier it was as if he was Yahweh
- JUSTIFIED BY WORKS: **Read James 2:18-26**. Abraham justified by works here? That’s what it says. But that’s what it says IN CONTEXT of imagining counter factual world of faith without works. What if Abraham had not offerred Isaac? Would God have stood by his promise? God knows all, so he chose the path where that question did not come up. Paul’s point holds firm: saved by faith, but James’ point holds firm too: there is no faith without works that demonstrate it. We want a legalistic formula for salvation, but the reality is that it’s a gift.
- GOD SWEARS BY HIMSELF: we read in Hebrews 6 that there was nothing greater for him to swear by
- DID GOD KNOW WHAT ABRAHAM WOULD DO?: if so, he tells us this is why he chose Abraham for the covenant. Because he was willing to sacrifice everything for God WHEN GOD ASKED HIM. Don’t get martyr syndrome and sacrifice when God doesn’t ask you to.
- REBEKAH’S LINE: Bethuel is father of Rebekah, wife of Isaac
