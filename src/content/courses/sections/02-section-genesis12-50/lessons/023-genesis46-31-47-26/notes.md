- NOW LET’S ZOOM ON JOSEPH/JESUS FRACTALS: Joseph Fractal Patterns (15 total):
  - HUMAN BROTHERS: Jesus’ human brothers, the Jews, plotted to kill him, thinking to keep their power and position with Rome. They thought he was dead, then miraculously they find him alive and ruling over everyone.
  - SPIRITUAL BROTHERS: Spiritual brothers (sons of God) aided in his crucifixion, threw him into the pit of Sheol as the great bulls of Bashaan that surrounded him in Psalm 22, expecting him to be defeated there, but God raised him up in a way they did not expect and put him over them
  - SAVING GENTILES: Jesus came first to the Jews, and the Gentiles were saved as a result because the Jews would not come to the marriage supper. Joseph says God sent him to Egypt to save the Israelites and the Gentile Egyptians were saved as a result.
  - ESCAPE TO EGYPT: Jesus was taken to Egypt by God as a child to escape being murdered by the King of Israel because of a prophecy that he would be king over Israel, and Joseph was taken to Egypt by God as a young man to escape being murdered by the sons of Israel because of dreams that prophesied he would be king over Israel.
  - GATHERING HARVEST AT 30: Joseph began gathering in the harvest in his primary position at age 30 in preparation for the seven years of famine and suffering. Jesus began his public ministry gathering the harvest of souls at age 30, seventh year of “plenty” ends about with Stephen’s martyrdom as the first act of violence against Jesus’s followers, in preparation for the final seven years of famine and suffering in Revelation.
  - NUMBERED WITH TWO TRANSGRESSORS, ONE SAVED AND ONE LOST: Joseph, like Jesus, is numbered with the transgressors though he had done nothing wrong. Two transgressors were with Jesus, one saved with him and one perished. Just like the Cup Bearer and the Baker with Joseph.
  - HOW CAN WE FIND A MAN LIKE THIS: Pharoah says of Jacob how can we find a man like this in whom is the spirit of God, just as the Pharisee asks who can forgive sins but God alone, or the disciples ask who is this man that even the wind and the seas obey him?
  - SILENT LAMB TO THE SLAUGHTER: Joseph was punished without complaint for the sins of others: his brother’s, Potiphar’s wife’s sins. Jesus was led like a lamb to the slaughter and opened not his mouth as he was punished for the sins of others.
  - SILVER SALE: Joseph was sold for 20 shekels of silver, Jesus for 30 pieces of silver
  - MIRACULOUS BREAD: Joseph fed thousands with miraculous bread. Jesus fed thousands with miraculous bread.
  - SERVANT LEADER: Joseph did not use his high position to make others serve him, instead he used it to serve others. Jesus said that he came not to be served, but to serve.
  - COMING PERSECUTION AND DELIVERANCE: Joseph and Jacob’s descendants knew there would be a long time of persecution coming because of God’s promise to Abraham. They were to stand firm through the fire, and when God finally delivers them back to the Promised Land, Joseph’s tells them to take his bones with them. Jesus predicted persecution for his name’s sake even through Revelation, but tells us to hold firm until he returns on the clouds of heaven with a new Earth and a new Heaven to deliver us from this present darkness.
  - SYMPATHIZE WITH THE STRANGER: God reminds Israel throughout the rest of the Bible that they are to love the stranger and treat them well because they were strangers in the land of Egypt. Joseph came so that Israel could empathize with strangers because they know what it was like to be a stranger in a foreign land. Hebrews tells us that Jesus became human so that he could sympathize with our condition as our priest and mediator with the father.

  - MESSIANIC PROTOTYPE: And most of all, Joseph is a singular messianic savior prototype. **Genesis 47:1-12 > Jacob meets Pharoah**

- PHAROAH ASKS ABOUT ELEPHANT IN THE ROOM: **so just how old are you**? Average life expectancy was 70-80ish. Jacob is 130, dies at 147. Hard to imagine. Our estimates of ages presuppose identical aging to today though
- SOJOURNING: Jacob, like Abraham and Isaac, saw himself as a visitor or a nomad, like a Gypsy. That’s why they could pick up and move to Egypt so quickly. They never made cities or put down roots.
- JACOB SEEMS A LITTLE CRANKY TO PHARAOH: he says I’m 130, my life stinks and always has, and I’m not as old as my fathers were. Goodbye.
- JACOB THE VICTIM: Jacob always seems to see him himself as a victim. As the secular Jewish commentator Robert Alter points out, in one sense **Jacob has gotten everything he ever wanted**. Many sons, he married his beloved Rachel, he got his father‘s blessing to take over the family, he had great wealth, he has God‘s promise, his favorite son is alive again. But in a testament to just how real the book of Genesis is, **he did not get them in the way that he wanted or expected,** so he is miserable and has been his whole life. As Dennis Prager pointed out, much of humanity could describe their life as Jacob described his.
- DAYS ARE EVIL: Hebrew only has one word (ra) for disagreeable, bad, hard, difficult, and moral evil. So it’s always translator’s choice how to render it.
- JACOB BLESSES PHAROAH: no way the Jews made that up after escaping Egyptian slavery.

**Genesis 47:13-26 > Joseph buys everything for Pharoah**

- PHAROAH OWNS EVERYTHING: Pharaoh was smart to let Joseph run it all. He literally ends up with ownership of every piece of money, every piece of land, and every body (animal and human) in Egypt and Canaan. It is hard to fathom that kind of power. It sets up Pharoah with the power to completely enslave the Hebrews later.
- TAKE FOR GRANTED THE HONESTY OF JOSEPH: he did not steal from Pharoah. He was one of the only non-corrupt successful leaders in all of human history. Unlike Satan in his rebellion against being subservient to God, Joseph was content to be under Pharoah. Don’t take this for granted: God’s meticulous plan required Joseph’s honesty or it might have all fallen apart, either because he got caught, or because he got richer than Pharoah, or because Pharoah didn’t end up head of a feudal state with power to enslave the Hebrews. Very, very few people in all of human history have ever been this honest in that position.
- LIVESTOCK FIRST: livestock eat grain, so if you’re out of food, you have to get rid of them first anyway. Now, pharaoh owns all the livestock. Who did pharaoh just put in charge of raising all of his livestock? Joseph’s family. **They now have all the livestock of Egypt in their control**..
- GENIUS OF JOSEPH’S 20% FLAT TAX: not actually terrible. We pay more today. But since he first sets it up as the people being slaves, when they only have to pay 20%, they are grateful for the 80. In the United States, the vast majority of people see taxes as a check they get from the government, so they look forward to tax time. Sneaky.
- PRIESTS ARE IMPORTANT: Ancient Egypt was ruled by Pharoah, but Pharoah needed support of the priesthood and the military, the two legs on which Pharoah’s power stands. That’s why the priests keep their land.
- WHAT GOES AROUND COMES AROUND INCLUSIOS:
  - SLAVERY: Joseph enslaves all of Egypt to pharaoh, and later a different pharaoh enslaves all of Joseph’s family.
  - MONEY: Joseph gave Pharoah all the money he earned at the stay of Israel’s enslavement, and later the Egyptians give it all back when Israel leaves.
