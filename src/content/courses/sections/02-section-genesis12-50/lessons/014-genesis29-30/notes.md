**Genesis 29:1-12 > Jacob meets Rachel**

- WOMEN INSPIRE MEN: how quickly Jacob is transformed from a mild man who dwells in tents to commanding shepherds what to do while rolling away the big stone to impress Rachel
- JACOB CRIES: and then after being the big tough man he cries after kissing her. Awkward.
- RACHEL’S PRINCESS STORY she grew up hearing about Aunt Rebekah’s princess story, and she dreamed of being the new Rebekah. She runs to tell her Dad, Laban…

**Genesis 29:13-20 >Jacob’s 7-year love for Rachel**

- ONE OF THE MOST ROMANTIC SENTIMENTS IN THE BIBLE: “And they seemed to him but a few days because of the love he had for her.” This was a non-sexual love. Jacob truly loved Rachel. Imaging the late nights up talking to each other, the nice things he would do for her, walks in the countryside, seven years of date nights and flowers. He truly loved Rachel.
- RACHEL IS SILENT: the Bible is 100% silent on whether Rachel loved Jacob back. Her entire life became consumed by rivalry with her sister, **making real love for Jacob likely impossible**, unable to detangle her feelings for Jacob from her feelings of competition and betrayal toward her sister.

**Genesis 29:21-30 > Jacob gets Leah and Rachel**

- DECEPTION GRADUATE SCHOOL: and Uncle Laban is his major professor.
- HOW? Apparently Leah minimized her speaking, and nights were pitch black before the invention of the light bulb. And Jacob was likely drunk given cultural practices.
- LIVE BY DECEPTION DIE BY DECEPTION: Can you blame Jacob for the mistake? I mean she was wearing a veil so he couldn’t see her, she brought meat, she was wearing goat fur so he thought it was Esau,…oh wait that is what HE did to his dad Isaac! Jacob also tried to flaunt the birthright rules of older sister/younger sister, at least according to Laban. **Jacob fell prey to a sibling swap deception.** Poetic justice.
- HOW COMPLICIT WAS LEAH? what did she think about the deception? Laban’s idea or hers? What did they do with Rachel? Tie her up?
- ANOTHER SIGN OF ANTIQUITY: marrying rival sisters is expressly prohibited in Leviticus 18:18: “And you shall not take a woman as a rival wife to her sister, uncovering her nakedness while her sister is still alive.”

**Genesis 29:31-end > Leah babies and Leah’s pain**

- BARREN MATRIARCHS: Every patriarch had this problem of wives that God made barren in order to show his miracle
- FEEL LEAH’S PAIN: with each passing name her expectations of her husband‘s love go down. She was deluding herself early that she could woo Jacob into loving her despite her looks. Women tends to define their own sense of worth by their looks just like men define their worth by their job or capability. **Feeling unattractive with no one to console is a very deep pit for a woman.** Leah thought Jacob would value her because of the boys. Instead, in spite of the boys, he still refused to love her. He left no question that it was entirely just her that he did not love, nothing outside of her.
- NAMES REFLECTING, DAD’S LACK OF LOVE FOR MOM: I cannot imagine the hell that this dysfunctional family went through. The children’s very names commemorated how dad not love mom and the feud between mom and your aunt. What a toxic environment Joseph was born into.
- LEAH IS A TRAGIC FIGURE: **Read Genesis 49:28-end.** Jacob doesn’t even call Leah his wife.
- THE ONLY PEOPLE WHO LOVE LEAH ARE HER CHILDREN. This may partly explain why women in those times were more interested in children than husbands. **The only unconditional male love they were going to find was from their sons.**
- TIMETABLE: we read this in 5 minutes and move on. This was the real day to day life of these unfortunate people for YEARS. Every day. Years.

**Genesis 30:1-13 > Rachel barren, Bilhah and Zilpah**

- RACHEL GIVE ME CHILDREN OR I SHALL DIE: sad irony is that she dies in childbirth on the second child
- FEEL WHAT RACHEL FEELS: Do you feel what Rachel feels? She wants to be a mother more than anything, but she can’t. And it’s killing her. And her vindictive sister reminds her of it every day both intentionally and just watching her with her children.
- THEORY MEN AND WOMEN READ THIS DIFFERENTLY: What Jacob hears Rachel say is not what she means. They need a translator between man-speak and woman-speak, like the monkey’s headband in “Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs.” Let’s test if my theory is true that men and women read this differently.
  - WHAT RACHEL MEANS: Rachel is desperate and depressed and lonely. I think what she means is “I’m depressed and at my rope’s end. I don’t know what to do but cry. And I’ve cried for years watching Leah with her boys every day and I’m exhausted with crying. Somebody, anybody, please help me. All I want is a son.”
  - WHAT RACHEL NEEDED FROM JACOB: What Rachel needed from Jacob was a hug in her anger and despair. Someone to tell her “This situation stinks, and it’s not your fault. You are loved even if you don’t have children.”
  - WHAT JACOB HEARD: “**I have a problem, and you have to fix it.**” Because when men hear a problem, it is involuntary that we look for solutions. We sympathize with other men by offering ideas to solve the problem. **Women hear our solutions not as sympathy but rather as discounting the value of their pain and invalidating their emotions**.
  - FOR MEN: WHAT DOES INVALIDATING EMOTIONS EVEN MEAN? For men, we learn early to mistrust our emotions. Our emotions tell us to rape, pillage, and plunder. We get it trouble very early on whenever we act on our emotions. Women’s emotions do not lead to such behavior and immediate correction, so they implicitly trust their emotions to get them through life. So if a woman says she feels something, that means she is sure of it and your responses or solutions are “invalidating” the feeling she is relying on to navigate life. If a man tells you he feels something, that often means he is unsure of it and is looking for guidance. **We are so different, but we do not have adequate language to communicate because we mean opposite things.** And Jacob, Rachel, and Leah are a peak inside the male/female dynamic.
  - JACOB FELT CORNERED: So the more Rachel’s tone put Jacob into an impossible position, the more she was really forcing Jacob into a corner where **he felt she was holding him responsible for an impossible situation**, he would lash out and hurt Rachel further. As Sun Tzu said in “The Art of War”, never back your enemy into a corner, always leave them an escape lest they become desperate.
  - RESULT IS BOTH ARE ANGRY AND ALONE: and feeling like the other doesn’t have a clue. And the cycle spirals ever downward. This family makes me very sad for them.
- MODERN VOLUNTARY RACHEL’S: we have an epidemic of middle-aged women who chose to be Rachel in their 20’s by prioritizing career and hoping the family works out later, only to find too late that the career was a bad husband and it starts to feel like all the good husbands are taken. Their anthem came out last year:

- IRONY OF WANTING WHAT WE DON’T HAVE: girls with curly hair wish they had straight hair, and girls with straight hair wish they had curly hair. Old men wish they were young again, and young boys in school wish they were old and free. What Rachel wanted was exactly what Leah had, and what Leah wanted was exactly what Rachel had. **They were architects of their own misery**: an entirely self-imposed desire making them miserable. Had they simply swapped desires between their brains, life would have been idyllic for both of them.

**Genesis 30:14-24 > Mandrakes and Joseph**

- LEAH’S IRONY: Leah is the one who stole Rachel’s husband in the first place. The perpetrator becomes the victim.
- MANDRAKES DON’T WORK: Leah conceives, not Rachel. God is making a point that magic doesn’t control the world, he controls the world. Torah explicitly says God opened Rachel’s womb later.

**Genesis 30:25-end > Jacob wealthy but trapped**

- GOD MADE STRIPES NOT THE STICKS: Jacob says next chapter that it was a dream he had and God did it, not magic sticks.
- IS WEALTH EVIL?: Jacob became wealthy. Isaac was wealthy. Abraham was wealthy. Solomon’s wealth was his downfall. Is money inherently evil? Should we voluntarily avoid earning? Jesus said **Luke 18:18-27** (rich young ruler and eye of needle). **Luke 16:19-31** (Lazarus and rich man). In the Lazarus story, there are two rich men in the afterlife. One is in torment in Hades, and one is “far off” in comfort with Lazarus (Abraham). **What was the difference? God.** All is possible with God. Abraham trusted God, the rich man did not. Money seems to act like a catalyst: if you are on a path away from God, money will take you there faster. If you are putting your trust in God first through the help of the Holy Spirit, then money can do great good like support your family with stability, support your church, help those in need, etc.

**Genesis 31:1-10 > Plans to leave and dream of spotted goats**

- PATH OF CAIN: it was because of God’s favor on Jacob that Laban became successful as well, but all his sons see is what they don’t have. They took the path of Cain to hate the success of others, even when the success of others was benefitting them!
- YOU ARE CAIN: and yet, you know the sons’ reaction is there inside you, too. Seeing Jacob get wealthier and wealthier all while he was supposed to be helping your Dad.
- SO GOD MADE THEM SPOTTED: it wasn’t sticks after all, unless God told him about the sticks as a visual sign to the people around Jacob.

**Genesis 31:11-13 > Angel of the LORD**

- ANGEL OF YAHWEH SAYS HE IS GOD: and specifically the God of Bethel
- GOD IS EVERYWHERE: As in, “I am here too, Jacob, not just at the “House of God” where you said ‘Surely God is in this place.’”

**Genesis 31:14-16 > Wives agree**

- PLANNING TO TAKE THE WEALTH: they are all on board with sneaking out all their stuff
- ONLY TIME LEAH AND RACHEL AGREE: this is the only time Rachel and Leah agree on something

**Genesis 31:17-32 > Rachel’s Theft and Laban’s search**

- WHY NEITHER GOOD NOR BAD?: why couldn’t Laban say anything good? Because Laban could have enticed Jacob back to live with him via flattery so he could continue to take advantage of him. God needed Jacob in Canaan, so Alana could neither hurt him nor entice him back. God outsmarted Laban: he left him no conniving way around God’s intention by including “good” in the prohibition.
- SYNCRETISM: Rachel seemed to believe both in pagan gods and in God. This is called “syncretism”. Dennis Prager puts it this way: “Rachel’s behavior may have been similar to that of Niels Bohr, the Nobel prize winning physicist who was said to keep a rabbit’s foot in his laboratory. When an astonished visitor asked “But surely professor you don’t believe in a rabbit‘s foot?” Bohr responded “Of course not. But they say a rabbit’s foot brings you luck whether you believe in it or not.”
- HONOR AMONG THIEVES: Laban didn’t believe Jacob because Laban knew he himself would have lied about it. The liar complains the loudest when he is lied to. The thief thinks stealing is fine until he is stolen from.
- CAN CUT THE TENSON WITH A KNIFE: imagine Rachel’s fear. What would Laban and Jacob have done?

**Genesis 31:33-end > Laban’s search**

- GREAT IRONY: Jacob has never lost his temper with Laban before despite many legitimate grievances. But he loses his temper here in the only case where Laban was actually right
- LABAN ADMITS. he was never going to allow Jacob to leave with his family or his possessions.
- JACOB AND LABAN AGREEMENT: it is an agreement between crooks. “God watch you or you’ll steal all my stuff”.
- RIGHTEOUS PUNISHMENT OF LIARS: As Prager writes: “This is the built-in punishment of the dishonest. They go through life convinced they are constantly being deceived.” In Laban’s case, his paranoia cost him his daughters and his grandchildren, and, ultimately, a share in God’s promise.
