**Genesis 12:1-3**

- ARBITRARY CHOICE: Abram’s selection seems arbitrary in the story. It doesn’t matter why he chose Abram, only what happened for the world after he did. Abram had great faith, but he had his problems too, just like the rest of us.
- UNKNOWN DESTINATION: Hall of Faith, Hebrews about Abraham who picked up and left, not even knowing where it was he was going to go
- NOT VAGUE FEELINGS: Note that Abram didn’t just have some vague feeling. God told him directly. And Abram believed him. Imagine working for Abram or being his family. “Guys, God told me to leave. Don’t know where….” Imagine Sarai living with him all those years: “God told me…”
- FAMILIES: “families” are blessed, not nations. How nice is that? God doesn’t care as much about nations as he does about families. Do you know how crazy it is that your family has been blessed through Abram, just as God said 4100 years ago?
- BLESS THOSE WHO BLESS YOU: Bless those who bless you, curse those who curse you: one of the most true prophecies in all of human history. Know any Hittites? Babylonians? Anybody think it’s great to be a Palestinian living in Gaza? Who blesses Israel the most? The US. Who is the most blessed nation? The US.
- REVOLUTIONARY BLESSING: nothing like this anywhere else then or now, that one nation will be a blessing to all others.

**Genesis 12:4-10**

- MAP: Ur -> Haran -> Canaan -> Shechem -> Bethel/AI -> Negeb -> Egypt
- SHECHEM: Mentioned in passing here, but a lot happens around that altar. God had a plan.
  - Abram builds it,
  - Jacob later builds his own altar there and buys land around it and digs a well,
  - Joshua gathered Israel there for rededication after returning to Promised Land
  - Rehoboam went there to be crowned king
  - Jesus sits there and talks to the Samaritan woman.
- FAMINE IN GOD’S PLAN: God said “go to the land I will show you,” and then there is a famine. God doesn’t promise life will be easy doing what he says.
- QUESTIONING ABRAM: Should Abram have stayed in Canaan? Just because someone did something in the Bible, doesn’t mean the Bible prescribes that action.

**Genesis 12:11-end**

- SITUATIONAL ABSOLUTE MORALITY: Was it right for Abram to ask Sarah to lie? Absolute morality is absolutely situational. In this case, Sarah would end up in the harem of the pharaoh either way. One choice has some chance of her being saved, and the other just has Abraham dead. However, the “so it may go well with me” could suggest a selfish ulterior motive
- TORAH SILENT ON ABRAM’S MORALITY: The Torah is silent on the rightness of Abram’s action. However, 1 Samuel 16:1-4 says God instructed Samuel to lie to the would-be murderer King Saul. Murderers are apparently not owed the truth. Choice between murder and lying, choose lying.
- HOW DID PHAROAH KNOW? I wonder who told Pharoah the reason for his disease?
- EXODUS PARALLEL: Uncanny: go there for famine, become prisoner, God punishes, then kicked out with wealth, back to Promised Land.

**Genesis 13:1-13**

- MAP: Negeb -> Bethel/Ai/Shechem -> Jordan Valley
- CLOSED CIRCUIT PUMP: Mount Hermon: 9000 ft above sea level, Sea of Galilee 700 ft below sea level, Dead Sea 1200 ft below sea level. Hot springs under sea north end of Galilee, that’s why Peter James John live on north shore: fish are in the warmer water. Evaporate from Dead Sea, condense as rain and snow on Mount Hermon.
- WEALTH IS NOT EVIL: Abram was wealthy: wealth is not inherently evil. There are good wealthy people and bad poor people. Wealth is more like a catalyst: it amplifies what you want to be.
- DID ABRAM KNOW?: Did Abram know sodom and Gomorrah were evil, or did he really give Lot the best ground as first choice?
- VIA MARIS VS. KING’S HIGHWAY: On the western mountain range, lot chooses the Jordan River Valley on the east side and Abraham has the part that is the Via Maris on the west side. As Napoleon said he who holds this land bridge, controls the power and wealth of the ancient world.

**Genesis 13:14-end**

- JEWS PROVE GOD: Frederick the Great, 18th century King of Prussia, was a skeptic of religion. He asked his court chaplain to give him a short proof of God’s existence. He gave him one word: “Jews”. There is no natural explanation for why the Jews are still a people. Know any Hittites? They were a huge empire. But they are gone. Jews had no homeland for 2000 years, and the world hates them and tries to kill them whenever they can.
- NO PARALLEL TO COVENANT: There is no parallel to the Abrahamic covenant. Just how unlikely do you think it is that of all the ancient texts that happened to survive, the only one that promises a homeland from God happens to be this one, and not only are these people still around, but they are the only ones who ever had a state there in history, and 2000 years after the last state they have a state now. **Hebrew is the ONLY dead language ever revived**, it was dead for nearly 2000 years. There literally is nothing close to this anywhere else.
