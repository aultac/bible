**Genesis 25:19-28 > Jacob born**

- HAIRY AND DECEIVER: Imagine being named “deceiver” or “cheater.” He was a baby!
- CRITERION OF EMBARRASSMENT: Jacob never comes across well in this story
- READ GOD’S MESSAGE TO REBEKAH MORE CLOSELY: notice it does NOT say anything about which child will receive his promise. Nobody learns that God chose Jacob for the promise until he talks to Jacob at Bethel in chapter 27.
- ISAAC LOVED ESAU: the only real mistake Isaac makes in this story. God hates Esau and loves Jacob. Isaac does not. And why? Food.

**Genesis 25:29-end > Birthright**

- SOLD FOR SOUP: We think of Jacob as conniving, but what about Esau’s complete disregard for the birthright? He sold it for soup? Food drives him just like his dad Isaac.
- ISAAC WAS NOT ON BOARD: Isaac tries to give it to Esau anyway. Was it even Esau’s to give? Did Isaac know?
- DID ANYBODY ASK GOD?: God had told Rebekah the older would serve the younger, and yet nobody later asked God what to do.
- DIDN’T MEAN IT: I personally don’t think Esau was serious about this, as evidenced by his reaction when Jacob actually takes it later. If you did this with your brother in your mid-teens, would you have ever thought Dad actually changes the will?

**Genesis 26:1-5 > famine but stay in Gerar**

- FIRST TALK WITH ISAAC: reassures him the promise is through him. Notice it does not say promise is only through Jacob.
- BIBLICAL FAITH = TRUST: once again, we see Isaac’s faith when God spoke not as “I believe you exist” (a tautology) but as “I believe you will do what you say.” Isaac shows strong faith like his father. Jacob does not. Jacob negotiates with God and wrestles with God. Isaac simply obeys and trusts.
- ONLY FAMINE WHERE EGYPT IS PROHIBITED: this is the only time God tells someone not to go to Egypt during famine.

**Genesis 26:6-11 > sister trick**

- **Hmmm…what would Dad have done?**
- DIFFERENT ABIMELECH: this reads to us like a guy named Abimelech is the most forgetful person on Earth. Just replace “Abimelech” with “Pharoah” in this story to see how this is a different person than before
- ABIMELECH DOES NOT DENY: he does not say “we would never do that”. He is only worried about himself. “Yes we would do that, but since you lied now look, I’m in trouble!” he is arguing that Isaac should care about him, but he should not have to care about Isaac.
- WARPED MORAL CODE: notice how they consider it terrible to sleep with another man’s wife while he’s alive, but **it’s just fine to murder him** to take her. Like the high priest who would not accept Judas’s money back because it was blood money, but he was fine to have murdered the man and made it blood money in the first place.
- LACK OF PHILISTINE SELF AWARENESS: you would think they might rethink their customs if this keeps happening to them. This does shed some light though: it is not only the King that does this. **The beautiful go to the powerful.** That was the culture, and it is still the culture in almost the entire Muslim world, especially with regard to non-Muslims: England 7k rapes in 2000 vs. 70k rapes in 2024. **Power determines morality in the non-Western world.**
- CRUELTY VS KINDNESS TO STRANGERS: Sodom and Gomorrah did this. Philistines did this. Benjamin did this in Judges. By contrast, Abraham was kind to stranger. Lot was kind to stranger. Rebekah was kind to stranger. See how the culture implicitly assumed the warped moral code was obviously correct, but **this revolutionary Torah sets up instead a God who says love the stranger rather than the beautiful go to the powerful.**
- MEASURING GOODNESS: LOVE THE STRANGER: remember the servant making goodness the criteria for Rebekah? How did he measure it? **How you treat people you don’t know who can do nothing for you.** Waiter, waitress, support line call center, concierge at hotel, other drivers on the road, etc. Gauge prospective friends and spouses this way. **Gauge yourself this way!**
- JESUS WAS A STRANGER: and look at what we did to him. And yet, he died for those who were cruel to him as a stranger. He showed the ultimate kindness to all of us strangers.

**Genesis 26:12-16 > go away**

- GO AWAY: they begged Abraham to stay so they would be blessed, but Isaac they sent away for fear of his retribution. Again, why did he fear them and lie if he was so powerful?
- EMULATE OR HONOR SUCCESS: Philistines chose the path of Cain, to envy the success and stop up the wells and send him away. As Dennis Prager put it, “Most people would rather stop up the wells of the world’s Abrahams than learn how to dig the wells.” “The Israel Test” by George Gilder was a fascinating look at separating the world this way with respect to Israel’s success. The minute the “Philistines” today stop spending all their efforts on killing Jews and instead focus on emulating or working with them, they will immediately begin to prosper.
- JEWISH TEMPLATE IN HISTORY: move in, become successful, surrounding people hate you and then try to expel or kill you

**Genesis 26:17-25 > wells and God’s oath**

- PREPARING FOR DESCENDANTS: Digging all these wells are a sign of his planning for descendants and towns on that land
- ISAAC IS NOT A FIGHTER: the Philistines challenge him, and he just moves on and digs another well
- GOD’S INTRODUCTION TO ISAAC: he does not say “I created the universe and you.” He says “I am the God of your father Abraham.” Apparently it is not readily obvious that this elohim is THE Elohim when he appears, but God is distinguishing himself from the other gods as the one with authority over Isaac because Abraham’s nation was his inheritance after Babel.
- GOD OF THE PROMISE: from Abraham forward, God identifies himself to humanity by his relationship to Abraham. Because the most important thing for all humans is that we all will be blessed through the promise to Abraham, so from there on out, he uses that as his identification. **I am the God who promised blessing to all through Abraham.**
- BECAUSE ABRAHAM OBEYED: remember how hard Abraham’s life was? No kids until 86, afraid for his life from Pharoah and Abimelech, Hagar and Sarah fighting, sent Hagar and Ishmael away with nothing, witnessed Sodom and Gomorrah, told to sacrifice Isaac. Do we ever say thank you? His faith enabled our salvation!! **THANK YOU ABRAHAM!**
- FEAR NOT: here it is again. God is scary in visions.
- GOD DOES NOT PICK THE PEOPLE THAT THE WORLD WOULD PICK: here we learned that God picks Jacob for the promise. Reading Jacob‘s story, none of us would have picked him. We would not have picked everyday-peasant-Galilean-fishermen to carry the Messiah’s message to the world. The world would have picked the first born, the strong man, the hunter. Not the deceiver who stayed in the tent with his mother. And yet this Torah shows us over and over again that God does not pick the first one as the world does. Isaac chosen over Ishmael, Jacob over Esau, Joseph over his brothers, Moses over Aaron, David over his brothers, Solomon over his brothers. The firstborn does have a place in God‘s prescribed Jewish rituals, he shows us that it doesn’t matter as much as we think it does. **God is the only one who carries water in broken vessels because that is how you know that he is acting: It is a miracle.**

**Genesis 26:26-33 > Abimelech treaty and Beersheba well**

- WE HAVE DONE NOTHING TO YOU: except harass you at every well you dug…

**Genesis 26:34-end > Esau’s wives**

- HITTITE WOMEN: remember how important it was to Abraham that Isaac not marry outside the family?
- MARRIAGE WITHIN FAMILY DOES NOT MEAN GOD WITHHOLDS PROMISE: Jacob’s son’s wives are largely not recorded, those that are were Canaanite and Egyptian, not Semitic. Yet they still all get the promise, and specifically through Perez, Judah’s offspring in a forbidden relationship between Judah and his dead son’s wife Tamar, assumed a Canaanite.
- HITTITE WOMEN INADVERTENTLY SAVE JACOB: they become the excuse to send him to Laban.

**Genesis 27:1-18 > Rebekah’s plan**

- REBEKAH’S PLAN: Jacob gets the blame usually, but this was clearly Rebekah’s plan.
- OLDER SON: Esau is referred to as his “older son” instead of “firstborn” because he sold the birthright for soup.
- HUNTING FOR FOOD NOT KOSHER: Later kosher prohibit killing an animal painfully, such as now and arrow. They must be killed with a single slice opening vein in neck so they die quickly and with minimal pain. That Jacob ate food from his favorite hunter son is proof of text’s ancient origin and truthfulness.
- A LOT IS LEFT OUT: How you interpret this story depends on those answers.
  - Did Isaac know of the birthright transfer?
  - Did Rebekah?
  - What was Rebekah’s motivation? Desire not to be lorded over by the Hittite wives when Esau took charge?
  - Did Rebekah tell Isaac what God told her about the children in her womb?
  - Did Isaac know that God told Rebekah the older will serve the younger?
  - If Isaac knew, did Isaac think that his blessing was contrary to God or just the usual blessing he would pronounce on the eldest?
  - Did Isaac think he had the power to confer God’s blessing?
  - Was God bound by Rebekah and Jacob’s deception?
- MAYBE INTERPRET KNOWING THAT WHAT IS LEFT OUT MUST NOT MATTER: Since God thought we could learn what we need without knowing those things, we can only learn what applies in both scenarios. The only version that works is that God might have chosen both, but in the end only chose Jacob and that had nothing really to do with Isaac. But Jacob got Isaac’s stuff because he became firstborn and he ruled household because of blessing.
- BIRTHRIGHT HAS NOTHING TO DO WITH GOD’S PROMISE: I was wrong about this my whole life. The birthright has nothing to do with God’s promise at all. All of Jacob’s sons inherit the promise. **When did God say only firstborns get Abraham’s promise?** God tells Jacob he gets the promise at Bethel. He might have told Esau that too if Esau hadn’t planned to kill Jacob. God chooses who he wants however he wants.
- SO WHY IS THE BIRTHRIGHT STORY HERE IF NOT RELATED TO GOD’S PROMISE?: it’s here to explain how Jacob ended up with Leah and Rachel from Haran. If you read it as Jacob assigning God’s promise, **there are a lot of open questions that the Bible gives no answers for**. But if you read it as unrelated to God’s promise, just narrative about how we got from A to B, then it makes perfect sense. Otherwise, you just get Jacob coming home from Laban terrified of Esau for no known reason and Esau has gone off and created his own people.

**Genesis 27:19-29 > Isaac blesses Jacob**

- WHEN READING THIS STORY TO CHILDREN: this is a good place to point out that just because someone in the Bible does something doesn’t mean it’s right or that the Bible is prescribing that you do the same thing. Jacob and Rebekah deceiving blind old Isaac is not to be emulated.
- DOES ISAAC ACTUALLY KNOW THIS WAS JACOB: why is he so suspicious?
- REBECCA WAS A GENIUS: Isaac used all four of his remaining senses to decide if it was Esau, hearing, taste, touch, and smell. Hearing was the only one that she couldn’t fix.
- DID THIS COME TRUE?: Mostly yes. Esau never bowed to him, but was under his lordship (as God predicted) until Esau left to start Edom. Edom did bow off and on.
- YOUR MOTHER‘S SONS: clearly calling out Jacob here. Ironically, if Isaac had other wives or concubines, this blessing would have restricted who he was in charge of.
- BLESS THOSE WHO BLESS YOU AND CURSED THOSE WHO CURSE YOU: we covered this before. This is one of the most accurate prophecies in all of human history.
- THE KEY PART OF ANY BLESSING WAS WHO IS GOING TO TAKE OVER THE FAMILY: we learned in Sunday school last year how the social structure of the ancient world was tribal and family-based. One son of the patriarch would become responsible for the rest of the family. This is the blessing that Isaac passes to Jacob. He is now in charge of the family.
- READ BLESSING CLOSELY: does it say anything about God’s promise to Isaac or Abraham?

**Genesis 27:30-38 > treachery discovered**

- WHY DOES ISAAC TREAT THE BLESSING AS IRREVOCABLE?: We want to explain this away via some legalistic explanation centered on ancient near eastern culture. But has this situation actually come up that often that it was enshrined in the common law? I don’t know the answer to this question.
- IT IS HARD NOT TO FEEL SYMPATHY FOR ESAU: what did he do wrong? Other than marrying the Hittite women, has he been a bad son? A bad brother? A cruel leader? A lazy worker? The only thing he did wrong was despise his birthright when he was hungry and tired. The rest of his woes came from Jacob.
- ONE THING ESAU DOES LACK: he is never recorded as having talked about God at all. He does not seem to look to God in his life or value him as he should.

**Genesis 27:38-39 > Esau’s blessing**

- WHY SO HARSH? Why did Isaac explicitly tell him that “away from the fatness of the earth shall your dwelling be? That doesn’t seem necessary or warranted for his favorite son who is weeping openly after just being cheated by his brother and mother.
- NOTICE THE FOOTNOTE: Replacing “away from” (ESV, NASB, NIV) with “of” (KJV) completely flips this from a curse to a blessing. Translation of a single word “_min_” that can legitimately mean both. Context doesn’t help: Esau tries to kill Jacob, implying curse, but he is the favored son so Isaac may have had compassion on him. Just not clear.
- CONTRAST “GIVE” WITH “WILL BE”: Contrasting with Jacob’s blessing, God gives to Jacob, but Esau’s blessing is just “is” or “will be” that he will “take” it with his sword. Success **given** by God vs. **taken** by force. So it could be “**Jacob’s was given by God, but your ‘fatness of the earth’ will be taken by your sword**.”
- DID THE BLESSING ACTUALLY COME TRUE? Hard to say, both in Esau‘s lifetime, nor in the later wars between Edam and Israel. Esau is under Jacob’s lordship until he leaves to start his own people, fulfilling God’s prophecy about them, but Jacob runs away nearly immediately. Esau ends up quite successful with many people and a powerful army. Edom is an arid and difficult land, however, so it may be that he did indeed live away from the fatness of the earth and the dew of heaven.
