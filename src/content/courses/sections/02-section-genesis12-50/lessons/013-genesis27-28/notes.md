\=> 20 mins reading

- WHEN READING THIS STORY TO CHILDREN: this is a good place to point out that just because someone in the Bible does something doesn’t mean it’s right or that the Bible is prescribing that you do the same thing. Jacob and Rebekah deceiving blind old Isaac is not to be emulated.
- WHY DID REBECCA DECEIVE ISAAC?: Does she hate Esau so much? take out the chapter breaks and reconsider the story as beginning where it says Esau married hit tight women. Consider Rebecca‘s position. After the crown passes from Isaac to Esau, **the Hittite women are now in charge of Rebecca**. Rebecca gets Jacob put in charge and then sends him off to find one of her nieces as a wife.
- THE STAGE IS SET: **Rebekah has covered 3 of the 4 of Isaac’s remaining senses**: taste, touch, and smell. Only hearing could not be fixed.

**Genesis 27:19-29 > Isaac blesses Jacob**

- DOES ISAAC ACTUALLY KNOW THIS WAS JACOB: why is he so suspicious?
- READ BLESSING CLOSELY: does it say anything about God’s promise to Isaac or Abraham?
- DID THIS COME TRUE?: Mostly yes. Esau never bowed to him, but was under his lordship (as God predicted) until Esau left to start Edom. Edom did bow off and on later, and eventually did become absorbed into Judah.
- YOUR MOTHER‘S SONS: clearly calling out Jacob here. Ironically, if Isaac had other wives or concubines, this blessing would have restricted who he was in charge of.
- THE KEY PART OF ANY BLESSING WAS WHO IS GOING TO TAKE OVER THE FAMILY: we learned in Sunday school last year how the social structure of the ancient world was tribal and family-based. One son of the patriarch would become responsible for the rest of the family. This is the blessing that Isaac passes to Jacob. He is now in charge of the family.

**Genesis 27:30-38 > treachery discovered**

- WHY DOES ISAAC TREAT THE BLESSING AS IRREVOCABLE?: We want to explain this away via some legalistic explanation centered on ancient near eastern culture. But has this situation actually come up that often that it was enshrined in the common law? I don’t know the answer to this question.
- IT IS HARD NOT TO FEEL SYMPATHY FOR ESAU: what did he do wrong? Other than marrying the Hittite women, has he been a bad son? A bad brother? A cruel leader? A lazy worker? The only thing he did wrong was despise his birthright when he was hungry and tired. The rest of his woes came from Jacob.
- ONE THING ESAU DOES LACK: he is never recorded as having talked about God at all. He does not seem to look to God in his life or value him as he should.

**Genesis 27:38-39 > Esau’s blessing**

- WHY SO HARSH? Why did Isaac explicitly tell him that “away from the fatness of the earth shall your dwelling be? That doesn’t seem necessary or warranted for his favorite son who is weeping openly after just being cheated by his brother and mother.
- NOTICE THE FOOTNOTE: Replacing “away from” (ESV, NASB, NIV) with “of” (KJV) completely flips this from a curse to a blessing. Translation of a single word “_min_” that can legitimately mean both. Context doesn’t help: Esau tries to kill Jacob, implying curse, but he is the favored son so Isaac may have had compassion on him. Just not clear.
- CONTRAST “GIVE” WITH “WILL BE”: Contrasting with Jacob’s blessing, God gives to Jacob, but Esau’s blessing is just “is” or “will be” that he will “take” it with his sword. Success **given** by God vs. **taken** by force. So it could be “**Jacob’s was given by God, but your ‘fatness of the earth’ will be taken by your sword**.”
- DID THE BLESSING ACTUALLY COME TRUE? Hard to say, both in Esau‘s lifetime, nor in the later wars between Edam and Israel. Esau is under Jacob’s lordship until he leaves to start his own people, fulfilling God’s prophecy about them, but Jacob runs away nearly immediately. Esau ends up quite successful with many people and a powerful army. Edom is an arid and difficult land, however, so it may be that he did indeed live away from the fatness of the earth and the dew of heaven. Eventually Idumea absorbed into Judah 125BC.

**Genesis 27:40-end > Esau’s plot**

- JACOB LOVED ESAU HATED: **read Malachi 1:2-4.**
- **WHY??** Maybe can make an argument why Esau hated (Esau did not care, spoke of murdering Jacob, never looked to God), but why is Jacob loved?
- IT IS GOD’S CHOICE: read **Romans 9:1-19**. At this point in history, the promise was God’s choice and he doesn’t tell us why, except maybe he knew what Abraham’s faith would be. There wasn’t a math formula that you follow like some occult ritual where you do X amount of good and now God chooses you. “But I want to be chosen like Abraham! No fair, I’d have just as much faith as him!” As of 32AD, God gave us a criteria that he would use to bestow the blessing on anyone, the fulfillment of the original promise to Abraham: **trust in his son and you will be saved.** Period.

**Genesis 28:1-5 > Isaac sends Jacob to Laban**

- WAIT SO NOW ISAAC BLESSES JACOB FOR REAL?: What a quick change.
- REBEKAH VANISHES: nothing more is heard from her after this. She is buried in the Tomb of the Patriarchs. We don’t know if she ever saw Jacob again.

**Genesis 28:6-9 > Esau adds a wife to one-up Jacob**

- DO NOT HAVE FAVORITES AMONG YOUR CHILDREN: Jacob repeats his parents mistake and they sell his favorite into slavery. Esau’s entire life is controlled by this favoritism.

**Genesis 28:10-end > Jacob’s ladder and Bethel**

- GOD’S CHOICE: has Jacob done anything to deserve this promise?
- BETHEL: Beth el: house of God. Becomes southern border of northern Israel 930 BC when civil war starts.
- WE KNOW 4 Hebrew words: “Beer" => well, “Beth" => house, “el” => God, “lechem” => bread
- JACOB IS CLEARLY UNCLE LABAN’S NEPHEW: “I will allow you to be my God if you give me all this stuff and I’ll even give you a 10% cut”.
- MIRRORS CALLING OF NATHANIEL: **Read John 1:43-end.** No deceit => Nathaniel reading Genesis 26, Jacob’s name is the deceiver, followed by Jacob’s ladder.

**Genesis 29:1-12 > Jacob meets Rachel**

- WOMEN INSPIRE MEN: how quickly Jacob is transformed from a mild man who dwells in tents to commanding shepherds what to do while rolling away the big stone to impress Rachel
- JACOB CRIES: and then after being the big tough man he cries after kissing her. Awkward.

**Genesis 29:13-20 >Jacob’s 7-year love for Rachel**

- ONE OF THE MOST ROMANTIC SENTIMENTS IN THE BIBLE: “And they seemed to him but a few days because of the love he had for her.” This was a non-sexual love. Jacob truly loved Rachel. Imaging the late nights up talking to each other, the nice things he would do for her, walks in the countryside, seven years of date nights and flowers. He truly loved Rachel.
- RACHEL IS SILENT: the Bible is 100% silent on whether Rachel loved Jacob back. Her entire life became consumed by rivalry with her sister, **making real love for Jacob likely impossible**, unable to detangle her feelings for Jacob from her feelings toward her sister.

**Genesis 29:21-30 > Jacob gets Leah and Rachel**

- HOW? Apparently Leah minimized her speaking, and nights were pitch black before the invention of the light bulb. And Jacob was likely drunk given cultural practices.
- LIVE BY DECEPTION DIE BY DECEPTION: Can you blame Jacob for the mistake? I mean she was wearing a veil so he couldn’t see her, she brought meat, she was wearing goat fur so he thought it was Esau,…oh wait that is what HE did to his dad Isaac! Jacob also tried to flaunt the birthright rules of older sister/younger sister, at least according to Laban. **Jacob fell prey to a sibling swap deception.** Poetic justice.
- DECEPTION GRADUATE SCHOOL: and Uncle Laban is his major professor.
- HOW COMPLICIT WAS LEAH? what did she think about the deception? Laban’s idea or hers?
- ANOTHER SIGN OF ANTIQUITY: marrying rival sisters is expressly prohibited in Leviticus 18:18: “And you shall not take a woman as a rival wife to her sister, uncovering her nakedness while her sister is still alive.”

**Genesis 29:31-end > Leah babies and Leah’s pain**

- BARREN MATRIARCHS: Every patriarch had this problem of wives that God made barren in order to show his miracle
- FEEL LEAH’S PAIN: with each passing name her expectations of her husband‘s love go down. She was deluding herself early that she could woo Jacob into loving her despite her looks. Women tends to define their own sense of worth by their looks just like me define their worth by their job or capability. **Feeling unattractive with no one to console is a very deep pit for a woman.** Leah thought Jacob would value her because of the boys. Instead, in spite of the boys, he still refused to love her.
- NAMES REFLECTING, DAD’S LACK OF LOVE FOR MOM: I cannot imagine the hell that this dysfunctional family went through. The children’s very names commemorated how dad not love mom and the feud between mom and your aunt. What a toxic environment Joseph was born into.
- LEAH IS A TRAGIC FIGURE: **Read Genesis 49:28-end.** Jacob doesn’t even call Leah his wife.
- THE ONLY PEOPLE WHO LOVE LEAH ARE HER CHILDREN. This may partly explain why women in those times were more interested in children than husbands. The only unconditional male love they were going to find was from their sons.
- TIMETABLE: we read this in 5 minutes and move on. This was the real day to day life of these unfortunate people for YEARS. Every day. Years.
