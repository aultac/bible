

**Final Summary: Genesis 1-11**

God created the heavens and the earth in six days and rested on the seventh. He created man and woman to steward His perfect creation and to commune with them in the Garden of Eden, where everything was good and perfect fellowship with God was experienced.

The serpent tempted them to rebel and choose knowledge over God's promise, to be like God knowing good and evil. The woman was deceived and Adam committed treason; they disobeyed, lost perfect fellowship, received consequences, were cursed, and driven from the garden. He could not allow them to eat from the Tree of Life, or they would live in their fallen state forever with no hope of redemption.

Sin ran rampant. Cain murdered Abel; sons of God took wives; every thought of man was totally evil, filled with pride, jealousy, and cruelty.

God was sorry He made man but found Noah righteous, from an undefiled line. Noah built the ark just as God commanded him and did everything else God commanded. God destroyed everything with a worldwide flood, saving only Noah, his family, and the animals on the ark.

The flood receded. Noah built an altar; the Lord was pleased with Noah's sacrifice and made a covenant never to flood the earth again, symbolized by a rainbow, even though man is always bent toward evil.

The nations descended from Noah's sons repopulated the earth, but mankind rebelled again, building the Tower of Babel as a monument to their greatness and to be like God. God confused their languages and scattered them over the earth according to the number of the sons of God.

Through Noah's descendants, God calls out Abram to begin the plan of salvation through a covenant.

**Summary: Genesis 1-2**

**Title:** **God creates the universe and man.**

**Storyline Summary:**

- God made everything good: Tohu v’vohu (formless and void) before creation, “fit for God’s purpose” after creation.
- Day 1: light
- Day 2: expanse the waters
- Day 3: land and seas and plants
- Day 4: sun moon stars
- Day 5: living creatures (animals)
- Day 6: mankind
- Day 7: God rested and made it Holy
- Macro view in Genesis 1 zooming in to the micro in Chapter 2
- “It is not good for man to be alone” – God builds woman as a helper who is his equal

**Interesting Points or Clarifications:**

- Parallelism in “god created man”
