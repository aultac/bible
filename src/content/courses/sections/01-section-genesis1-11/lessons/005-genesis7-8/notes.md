**Genesis 6:11-13** **:** 1:30

- IS GOD UNJUST? People call God unjust, but that’s only because the Bible is not a pornographic book. It did not detail the evil of man. Given all the evil that we know of today for which God did NOT destroy everything, how evil it must’ve been then. How many of those same people who accused God of unrighteousness are also angry at God for not giving Hitler a heart attack?
- GOD DOESN’T JUST READ ABOUT PAIN: Remember that God is the most tragic figure in the Bible. He knows every hair on your head. He feels every pain of the tortured. The rest of us just read about them. He knew every pain of those who drowned.
- GOD DID NOT REMOVE FREE WILL: God could have responded to mankind‘s evil by taking away free will. Instead, he moved from one rule to conscience to all the sending from a good man in his generation to moral revelation to everyone, then to just one group
- GOD’S ACTIONS ARE RIGHTEOUS: You SHOULD have wanted God to do this. Because God is righteous.

**Genesis 6:13-18**: 0:40

- WHAT IS ARK: What is an ark? Three things in scripture: Ark of Covenant, basket that Moses was in, and Noah’s boat. An ark is something steered by God.
- ANIMALS DIE, CLEANSE SIN: Why did the animals have to die? Nature has no purpose without man AND man’s sin had contaminated the physical space. The flood waters and death cleansed the earth.
- COVENANT: God makes a covenant with Noah. A contract. God does not do this very often. Appreciate what this means: no matter what, this thing will happen. You can bet on it. This is what “faith” (trust) in God is about: that he **will** do what he says.

**Genesis 6:19-7:3**: 2:15

- CLEAN VS. UNCLEAN: Which animals were clean and which were unclean prior to Leviticus? Abel raised animals, it would seem they were eaten before God’s command in Genesis.
- CONTRADICTION: Believe it or not, people have pointed to this as a Bible contradiction: 7 pairs vs. 1 pair.
- ANIMALS FIT: Story of the professor I gave a talk with in the Netherlands: do you really believe that all the animals in the world were on a big boat? This is about the size of 450 semi trucks. All 4,000 of my cattle full-grown fit in 114 semi trucks. Answers in Genesis people say about 7000 animals. Plenty of room. The real answer to this person is the “regular universe is required for both science and miracles”: why in the world is it difficult to believe anything that the creator of the universe did as a miracle?
- 500 FLOOD STORIES: Nearly every record of history we have from every culture has a flood story, is it not more likely that there was a flood of some kind?
- CONTRACT WITH GILGAMESH: The gods destroyed the earth because the people were too noisy. They save people because they are powerful or on their side against other gods. The gods could not stop the floods they unleashed and became scared of it. Then they were so relieved when Utnapishtim survived the flood and sacrificed food to them because they were hungry and needed his sacrifice. Yahweh destroyed the Earth because it was wicked. And he saved Noah because he was righteous.

**Genesis 8:5-end**: 1:30

- PLEASING AROMA: Contrast God smelling the pleasing aroma of the sacrifices with the other Mesopotamian flood stories where the gods had been deprived of human sacrifices, and they swarmed them like flies after the flood was over. Those gods were dependent on humans. This God is completely different than any God they knew. A big part of the Torah’s purpose is to introduce humanity to the God completely unlike any other.
- ANTHROPOMORPHIZING GOD: Anthropomorphizing God: The text makes it sound like God said he would never destroy the world again because of the pleasing aroma of the sacrifice. God does not have a nose, he does not have an appetite. **The God of the Torah was different from all other Gods: he does not eat.** The “showbread” in the tabernacle is a daily reminder that God does not eat. The other Gods needed the humans to sacrifice after the flood because they were dependent on the humans to provide their food.
- GOD CHOOSES TO SMELL: Just like God “chooses” to remember, he “chooses” to regret making man and God “chooses”: to smell the pleasing aroma: we want him to, and if he accepts our sacrifice, then he will choose to smell the pleasing aroma because that is what we are asking him to do. Don’t lame and crippled animals smell the same as unblemished ones? **Malachi 1:6-16**
