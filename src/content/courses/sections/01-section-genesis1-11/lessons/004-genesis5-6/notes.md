**Chapter 6: START 9:34, reading 3:00 + 9 mins other reading + 13 mins commentary = 25 mins, DONE BY 9:59**

**Verses 1-5: (8 mins)**

- What I am about to teach you from the Bible is not what I wanted to believe. But as Dennis Prager said in a debate with Alan Dershowitz, when you disagree with the Torah, you assume that the Torah is wrong and when I disagree with the Torah, I assume that I am wrong.
- Do you believe what your Bible says?

- NEPHILIM: There are storylines in the Bible that are self-contained, and then there are those that span long sections of text. Nephilim are one of those storylines.
- “Nephilim” literally comes from “to fall” or “fallen ones”
- Genesis 6: what are Nephilim?
- Nephilim: Numbers 13:33. Anak was one of their great parents, the “Anakim” were his descendants. Goliath was most likely Anakim. All the “giants” were known as “Rephaim”. They settled in Canaan. Joshua drives them all out EXCEPT for the Philistine cities.

- SONS OF GOD: Genesis 6: what are sons of God?
- The options out there for the sons of God are: the Royal line of rulers, the sons of Israel, the sons of Seth with the daughters of Cain, or the divine council view.
- READ: Job 1:6, 2:4, Job 38
- No humans were around in Job 38. No humans would impart supernatural hugeness to their descendants. You would **only** try to make them human if you had a predisposition against the spiritual realm apart from the text itself. Which I used to have.

- BAD SONS OF GOD?
- READ: Jude 1:5-9
- Notice the archangel did not have the authority to pronounce judgment on one of Satan’s rank, so he says “The Lord Rebuke You” instead of “I rebuke you.”
- We modern Christians tend to call all Elohim “angels” like we call all tissue “Kleenex”. But “angel” is a function or job (messenger) not what they are. 1 and 2 Peter also talk about these guys
- so why are these sons of God bad? Didn’t God make them good? Should sons of God be good? Jesus said to the rich young ruler. “Why do you call me good? **There is none good but God**“. They were placed in a position of God like authority and became drunk on their own power.
- READ: Psalm 82
- The only “son of God” that turned out Good was the only one that was a part of the Trinity, i.e. God himself as his own son.

- DIVINE COUNCIL: God rules with a divine council in heaven.
- READ (3 mins): 1 Kings 22:19 Divine Council ruling the world
- Skip: Daniel 10:13-21

- GODS OF THE NATIONS: Deuteronomy 32:1-9
- Conclusion: the gods of the nations were real. They were high-ranking members of the heavenly host known as “sons of God” who rebelled against God when put in positions of God-like authority.

- CHRONOCENTRISM: Think about it: **every person, culture, and civilization believed in a god** of some kind except a small minority of people in the West in the last 200-300 years. Who’s wrong: us or them? Turns out they knew what they were talking about. We are in a spiritual battle. And one side is happy for you to not even know they exist.

- STAY FOCUSED ON GOD: God makes this a minor story so that we will focus more on him than on the demons. C. S. Lewis: “There are two equal and opposite errors into which our race can fall about the devils. One is to disbelieve in their existence. The other is to believe, and to feel an excessive and unhealthy interest in them. They themselves are equally pleased by both errors and hail a materialist or a magician with the same delight.”

**Verses 5-8: (5 mins commentary)**

- GOD IS SAD: Who is the most tragic figure in the Old Testament? God. His children all rejected him. He even came and died for them and a lot of them still rejected him. God does not destroy the world in anger, instead he destroys it in sadness.
- HE KNOWS US: Think about it: he is not just reading about historic evil like the Holocaust, he was there, suffering right along with those who were being tortured. He knows their pain. He knows every hair on their head.
- Most ancient cultures have a flood story. Many of them have a single person being saved. But only this one is destroyed the world because of evil and saved the man because he was GOOD. Others were because they were powerful or wealthy, or half-gods. Consider the Epic of Gilgamesh: the gods saved Utnapishtim, destroying the people of the earth in a flood because they were making too much noise for the gods to sleep.
- WE ARE SAVED TO BE RIGHTEOUS. The moral of the story is that **God hates evil**. He cares about goodness. Not how handsome someone is, not how rich someone is, not how powerful someone is, not how pious someone is. He cares about goodness.
- HITLER HEART ATTACK? And this God cares about goodness, humans accuses of being cruel in the flood and yet those same people accused God of being unjust when he didn’t give Hitler a heart attack.
- HARDER TO BE GOOD SURROUNDED BY BAD? Was Noah righteous in general (even though his generation was bad: is this harder or easier?) or only compared to his generation. Compare Noah to Abraham who argued with God over destroying Sodom. Another meaning could be that we do not judge people by the standards of another age.
